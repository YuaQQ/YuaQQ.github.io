## Water & Solutions

- Water is a polar molecule with a low degree of dissociation.
- **Acidic solutions**: High H⁺ concentration; acids donate H⁺ ions (e.g., HCl → H⁺ + Cl⁻).
- **Basic solutions**: Bases release OH⁻ ions or accept H⁺ ions (e.g., NaOH → Na⁺ + OH⁻).
- **pH scale**: Measures solution acidity/basicity; pH = -log[H⁺].
- **Neutral solution**: [H⁺] = [OH⁻] = 10⁻⁷ M, pH = 7.

## Amino Acids

- Proteins are built from 20 amino acids, differing only in side chains (R groups).
- **General structure**: Central carbon (α-carbon) bonded to amino group (-NH₂), carboxyl group (-COOH), hydrogen atom, and R group.

## Amino Acid Classification by Side Chain

### Nonpolar Amino Acids (Hydrophobic)

- Usually buried inside water-soluble proteins or within transmembrane domains.
- **Glycine (Gly, G)**
- **Alanine (Ala, A)**
- **Valine (Val, V)**
- **Leucine (Leu, L)**
- **Isoleucine (Ile, I)**
- **Methionine (Met, M)**
- **Phenylalanine (Phe, F)**
- **Tryptophan (Trp, W)**
- **Proline (Pro, P)**

### Polar Amino Acids (Hydrophilic)

- Usually on the surface of water-soluble proteins or facing aqueous environments in transmembrane proteins.
- Common functional groups: sulfhydryl (-SH), hydroxyl (-OH), amide (-CONH₂).
- **Serine (Ser, S)** – common phosphorylation site.
- **Threonine (Thr, T)** – common phosphorylation site.
- **Cysteine (Cys, C)** – can form disulfide bonds.
- **Tyrosine (Tyr, Y)** – common phosphorylation site.
- **Asparagine (Asn, N)**
- **Glutamine (Gln, Q)**

### Charged Amino Acids

- Usually on protein surfaces or facing aqueous environments.
- **Acidic (Negatively Charged)** – contain carboxyl group (-COOH) in side chain:
    - **Aspartate (Asp, D)**
    - **Glutamate (Glu, E)**
- **Basic (Positively Charged)** – contain amino group (-NH₂/NH₃⁺) in side chain:
    - **Lysine (Lys, K)** – common acetylation site in histones.
    - **Arginine (Arg, R)**
    - **Histidine (His, H)**

## Amino Acid Propensities in Secondary Structures

- **Alpha helix (right-handed)**: Frequently contain M, A, L, E, K, Q (mnemonic: MALEKQ).
- **Beta sheet**: Frequently contain W, T, F, I, Y, V (mnemonic: WTFIYV).
- **Turns and loops**: Frequently contain Glycine (G) and Proline (P).

## Amino Acid Titration & Zwitterions

- Amino acids are **zwitterions**: molecules with both positive and negative charges.
- In aqueous solution:
    - Carboxyl group (-COOH) is usually deprotonated (-COO⁻).
    - Amino group (-NH₂) is usually protonated (-NH₃⁺).
- Net charge depends on pH relative to pKa values of functional groups.

## Ion-Exchange Chromatography

- **Purpose**: Protein purification based on net charge.
- **General process**:
    1. Protein mixture loaded onto a column with charged resin.
    2. Proteins bind to resin with opposite charge.
    3. **Elution**: Proteins are released by changing buffer conditions.
- **Elution methods**:
    - **pH gradient**: When buffer pH reaches protein's pI, protein becomes neutral and elutes.
    - **Salt gradient**: Increasing salt concentration competes with protein for resin binding sites; less charged proteins elute first.
- **Cation-exchange chromatography**:
    - Resin: Negatively charged.
    - Binds: Positively charged proteins.
    - Elution: Increase pH to pI of target protein.
- **Anion-exchange chromatography**:
    - Resin: Positively charged.
    - Binds: Negatively charged proteins.
    - Elution: Decrease pH to pI of target protein.
- **Key condition for binding**: The pH of the mobile phase must be between the pI of the target protein and the pKa of the resin's functional group.
