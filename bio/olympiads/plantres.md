# Plant Responses

### **Signal Transduction in Plants**

- **Reception → Transduction → Response**.
- Example: Light-induced de-etiolation (greening) in potatoes.

### **Tropisms: Directional Growth Responses**

- **Positive Tropism**: Growth toward stimulus.
- **Negative Tropism**: Growth away from stimulus.
- **Types**:
    - **Gravitropism**: Response to gravity (roots: positive; shoots: negative).
    - **Phototropism**: Response to light (shoots: positive; roots: negative).
    - **Thigmotropism**: Response to touch (e.g., tendrils coiling).

### **Plant Hormones**

- **Auxin (IAA)**:
    - Produced in shoot tips; promotes cell elongation.
    - **Polar transport**; asymmetric distribution causes phototropism/gravitropism.
    - **Mechanism**: Stimulates proton pumps → acidifies cell wall → activates **expansins** → wall loosening → cell elongation.
    - Controls apical dominance; involved in fruit development.
- **Cytokinins**:
    - Produced in roots; promote cell division.
    - Work with auxin to control differentiation (high cytokinin: shoots; high auxin: roots).
    - Delay senescence; antagonize apical dominance.
- **Gibberellins**:
    - Promote stem elongation, seed germination, fruit growth.
    - In germination: Signal **aleurone** layer to hydrolyze endosperm starch.
- **Abscisic Acid (ABA)**:
    - Stress hormone; promotes stomatal closure, seed dormancy.
    - Antagonizes growth hormones.
- **Ethylene**:
    - Gaseous hormone; promotes fruit ripening, senescence, leaf abscission.
    - **Triple Response** to mechanical stress: slows elongation, thickens stem, induces horizontal growth.
- **Brassinosteroids**:
    - Promote cell elongation and division; similar effects to auxin.

### **Light Responses and Photoreceptors**

- **Photomorphogenesis**: Light effects on plant morphology.
- **Photoreceptors**:
    - **Cryptochromes** (blue light): Control hypocotyl elongation, stomatal opening, phototropism.
    - **Phytochromes** (red/far-red light): Control de-etiolation, seed germination, shade avoidance, flowering.
- **Phytochrome Isomers**:
    - **Pᵣ** (inactive, red-absorbing).
    - **Pᶠʳ** (active, far-red-absorbing).
    - Reversible by light; conversion marks day/night cycles.

### **Biological Rhythms and Photoperiodism**

- **Circadian Rhythms**: ~24-hour cycles; entrained by light/dark.
- **Photoperiodism**: Response to day/night length.
- **Critical Night Length**: Determines flowering response.
    - **Short-Day Plants**: Flower when night length > critical value (flower in fall/spring).
    - **Long-Day Plants**: Flower when night length < critical value (flower in summer).
    - **Day-Neutral Plants**: Flower independent of photoperiod.
- **Phytochrome Role in Flowering**:
    - **Pᶠʳ inhibits flowering in short-day plants**.
    - **Pᶠʳ promotes flowering in long-day plants**.

### **Plant Defenses Against Pathogens**

1. **First Line**: Physical barriers (epidermis, periderm).
2. **Second Line: PAMP-Triggered Immunity**:
    - Recognition of **Pathogen-Associated Molecular Patterns (PAMPs)**.
    - Responses: **Phytoalexin** production, cell wall toughening.
3. **Third Line: Effector-Triggered Immunity**:
    - **R genes** encode R proteins that detect pathogen effectors.
    - **Hypersensitive Response**: Localized cell death to restrict pathogen.
    - **Systemic Acquired Resistance**: Long-lasting, whole-plant resistance via signaling molecules (e.g., salicylic acid).
