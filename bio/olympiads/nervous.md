# Nervous System

## Nervous System Overview

- Divided into **Central Nervous System (CNS)** and **Peripheral Nervous System (PNS)**.
- **CNS** includes brain and spinal cord.
- **PNS** includes nerves, ganglia, and nerve plexuses outside CNS.

## Central Nervous System (CNS)

- **Brain** and **spinal cord**.
- Contains **gray matter** (neuron cell bodies, dendrites, unmyelinated axons) and **white matter** (myelinated axons).
- **Central canal** of spinal cord and **ventricles** of brain are filled with cerebrospinal fluid.

## Peripheral Nervous System (PNS)

- Consists of **nerves** and **ganglia**.
- Transmits information to and from CNS.
- Includes:
    - **Cranial nerves** (12 pairs) – originate in brain, serve head and upper body.
    - **Spinal nerves** (31 pairs) – originate in spinal cord, serve body below head.
- **Afferent (sensory) neurons** carry information to CNS.
- **Efferent (motor) neurons** carry information away from CNS.

## Functional Divisions of PNS

- **Somatic nervous system**: voluntary control of skeletal muscles.
- **Autonomic nervous system (ANS)**: involuntary control of cardiac muscle, smooth muscle, glands.
    - **Sympathetic division**: "fight-or-flight" responses.
    - **Parasympathetic division**: "rest-and-digest" responses.
    - **Enteric division**: controls digestive tract, pancreas, gallbladder.

## Autonomic Nervous System (ANS) Effects

- **Parasympathetic**:
    - Constricts pupil, stimulates saliva, constricts bronchi, slows heart, stimulates digestion and urination, promotes erection.
- **Sympathetic**:
    - Dilates pupil, inhibits saliva, dilates bronchi, accelerates heart, stimulates adrenaline release, inhibits digestion and urination, promotes ejaculation.

## Nervous System Cells

- **Neurons**:
    - Excitable cells generating and transmitting action potentials.
    - Structural types:
        - **Pseudounipolar**: sensory neurons.
        - **Bipolar**: retina neurons.
        - **Multipolar**: motor neurons (most common).
    - Parts: dendrites, cell body, axon, synaptic terminals.
- **Neuroglia (glial cells)**:
    - **PNS**: Schwann cells (myelination), satellite cells (support ganglia).
    - **CNS**: oligodendrocytes (myelination), microglia (phagocytosis), astrocytes (environment regulation), epitympanic cells (line ventricles).

## Neuron Signaling

- **Resting potential**: ~-70 mV, maintained by K⁺ leak channels and Na⁺/K⁺ pump.
- **Action potential**:
    - All-or-none depolarization to ~+40 mV.
    - Stages: resting, threshold, depolarization (Na⁺ in), repolarization (K⁺ out), hyperpolarization, return to rest.
- **Conduction**:
    - Unidirectional due to refractory period.
    - In myelinated axons, saltatory conduction between nodes of Ranvier.

## Synapses

- **Chemical synapse**:
    - Neurotransmitter released from presynaptic neuron, crosses synaptic cleft, binds receptors on postsynaptic neuron.
    - Steps: AP arrival → Ca²⁺ influx → vesicle fusion → neurotransmitter release → receptor binding → ion channel opening → postsynaptic potential.
- **Electrical synapse**:
    - Gap junctions allow direct ion flow between cells (e.g., cardiac muscle, some brain regions).
- **Neurotransmitter effects**:
    - **Excitatory** (e.g., glutamate): opens Na⁺ channels, promotes depolarization.
    - **Inhibitory** (e.g., GABA, glycine): opens K⁺ or Cl⁻ channels, promotes hyperpolarization.

## Major Neurotransmitters

- **Acetylcholine**: muscle contraction, parasympathetic effects; broken down by acetylcholinesterase.
- **Biogenic amines**:
    - **Serotonin**: mood, sleep (from tryptophan).
    - **Dopamine, norepinephrine, epinephrine**: movement, alertness, stress response (from tyrosine).
- **Amino acids**: GABA (inhibitory), glutamate (excitatory), glycine (inhibitory).
- **Neuropeptides**: substance P (pain), endorphins (pain relief).
- **Gases**: NO (vasodilation, erection), CO (neurotransmission modulation).

## Brain Structure & Function

- **Forebrain**:
    - **Cerebrum**: higher functions (thought, memory, emotion).
    - **Cerebral cortex**: four lobes (frontal, parietal, temporal, occipital) with sensory, motor, and association areas.
    - **Corpus callosum**: connects hemispheres.
    - **Basal nuclei**: movement planning.
    - **Thalamus**: sensory/motor relay station.
    - **Hypothalamus**: homeostasis, endocrine control, circadian rhythms.
    - **Limbic system**: emotion, memory (includes amygdala, hippocampus).
- **Midbrain**: sensory integration, visual/auditory reflexes.
- **Hindbrain**:
    - **Pons**: breathing regulation.
    - **Medulla oblongata**: vital reflexes (breathing, heart rate, vomiting).
    - **Cerebellum**: coordination, balance, motor learning.

## Spinal Cord

- Continuation of medulla oblongata.
- 31 pairs of spinal nerves.
- **Gray matter**: sensory (posterior horn) and motor (anterior horn) neurons.
- **White matter**: ascending/descending tracts.
- **Reflex arcs**: rapid, involuntary responses (e.g., stretch reflex, tendon reflex).

## Sensory Systems

- **Receptors**:
    - **Mechanoreceptors**: touch, pressure, sound.
    - **Chemoreceptors**: taste, smell.
    - **Photoreceptors**: light.
    - **Thermoreceptors**: temperature.
    - **Nociceptors**: pain.
- **Sensory pathway**: reception → transduction (receptor potential) → transmission (action potentials) → perception (brain processing).

## Special Senses

- **Hearing**:
    - Sound waves → eardrum → ossicles → oval window → cochlear fluid → basilar membrane → hair cells → auditory nerve → brain.
    - Pitch (frequency) and volume (amplitude) encoded.
- **Equilibrium**:
    - **Utricle/saccule**: linear acceleration, gravity.
    - **Semicircular canals**: angular acceleration.
- **Taste (gustation)**:
    - Taste buds detect sweet, sour, salty, bitter, umami.
- **Smell (olfaction)**:
    - Olfactory receptors in nasal epithelium detect odorants.
- **Vision**:
    - Light → cornea → pupil → lens → retina (rods/cones) → optic nerve → brain.
    - **Rods**: low-light, monochrome.
    - **Cones**: color, detail (concentrated in fovea).
    - **Visual pigment**: retinal + opsin (rhodopsin in rods).

## Clinical & Functional Notes

- **Upper motor neuron (UMN) damage**: spasticity, hyperreflexia, Babinski sign.
- **Lower motor neuron (LMN) damage**: flaccidity, hyporeflexia, atrophy.
- **Blood-brain barrier**: tight junctions in capillaries protect CNS, restrict drug passage.
- **Multiple sclerosis**: demyelination in CNS slows action potentials.
- **Botulism**: neuromuscular junction blockade (prevents ACh release).
- **Vision correction**: concave lens for myopia, convex lens for hyperopia.
