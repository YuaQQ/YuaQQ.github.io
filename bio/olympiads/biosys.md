# Evolutionary History of Organisms

## Phylogenetic groupings

- A **monophyletic** grouping consists of an ancestral species and all of its descendants.
- A **paraphyletic** grouping consists of an ancestral species and some of the descendants.
- A **polyphyletic** grouping consists of various species that lack a common ancestor.

## Morphology evolution and phylogeny

- **Apomorphy** (Derived Trait): A specialized trait that distinguishes a group from its ancestor.
    - E.g. Feathers are an apomorphy of birds.
- **Plesiomorphy** (Ancestral Trait): A primitive trait inherited from a distant ancestor.
    - E.g. Reptiles are ectothermic, a trait inherited from their ancestors.
- **Synapomorphy** (Shared Derived Trait): A derived trait shared by two or more taxa, indicating a common ancestor.
    - E.g. Mammary glands are a synapomorphy for mammals.
- **Autapomorphy** (Unique Derived Trait): A derived trait unique to a single taxon.
    - E.g. The ability to speak in humans.
- **Homoplasy** (Convergent/Parallel Trait): Similar traits in unrelated organisms resulting from convergent evolution, not shared ancestry.
    - E.g. Wings in birds and bats.

## Viruses

- Noncellular, infectious particles that replicate only inside living cells
- Considered either very simple microorganisms or very complex molecules
- Shapes: helical, polyhedral, spherical, bacteriophage
- **Structures**:
    - **Genomes**: Double- or single-stranded DNA, or double- or single-stranded RNA
    - **Capsid**: Protein shell enclosing the viral genome, built from capsomeres
    - Some have **envelopes** (membranous coverings) to aid infection (e.g., influenza virus)
- **Replication**:
    - **Bacteriophage**: Infect bacteria; replicate via lytic or lysogenic cycles
    - **Retrovirus** (e.g., HIV): RNA reverse transcribed into dsDNA, integrated into host genome as a **provirus**
- **Viral Agents**:
    - **Viroids**: Circular RNA molecules infecting plants
    - **Prions**: Infectious proteins causing brain diseases (e.g., mad cow disease); propagate by converting normal proteins

### Bacteria & Archaea

- **Prokaryotes**
- Three common shapes: spheres (cocci), rods (bacilli), spirals
- **Reproduction & Gene Transfer**:
    - Asexual: **Binary fission**
    - Horizontal gene transfer: **Transformation** (DNA from environment), **Transduction** (via virus), **Conjugation** (direct plasmid transfer)
- **Nutritive Modes** (based on carbon & energy sources):
    - **Photoautotrophs**: Use light & CO₂ (e.g., cyanobacteria)
    - **Chemoautotrophs**: Use chemicals & CO₂ (e.g., deep-sea prokaryotes)
    - **Photoheterotrophs**: Use light & organic compounds (e.g., heliobacteria)
    - **Chemoheterotrophs**: Use organic compounds for both carbon & energy (most bacteria/archaea)
    - Oxygen requirements: **Obligate aerobes** (require O₂), **Obligate anaerobes** (poisoned by O₂), **Facultative anaerobes** (survive with/without O₂)
- **Bacterial Lineages**:
    - **Proteobacteria**: Largest, most diverse (e.g., E. coli, Rhizobium)
    - **Spirochetes**: Spring-shaped; cause syphilis, Lyme disease
    - **Chlamydias**: Live inside eukaryotic cells; lack peptidoglycan (e.g., Chlamydia trachomatis)
    - **Cyanobacteria**: Photoautotrophs
    - **Gram-positive bacteria**: Thick peptidoglycan layer (e.g., Staphylococcus)
- **Gram Staining**: Distinguishes bacteria by cell wall composition
- **Pathogens & Toxins**:
    - **Endotoxin**: Lipopolysaccharide from gram-negative cell walls
    - **Exotoxin**: Secreted by living bacteria (e.g., botulinum toxin)
- **Archaea**:
    - Share traits with bacteria and eukaryotes
    - **Extremophiles**: Thrive in extreme conditions (halophiles—salty, thermophiles—hot)
    - **Methanogens**: Release methane; live in moderate environments

### Protists

- Paraphyletic eukaryotic group; mostly unicellular, some colonial/multicellular
- **Supergroups**:
    - **Excavata**: Flagellated; includes diplomonads, parabasalids, euglenozoans
    - **SAR Supergroup**: Includes stramenopiles (diatoms, brown algae), alveolates (ciliates, dinoflagellates), rhizarians (radiolarians, foraminiferans)
    - **Archaeplastida**: Includes red algae, green algae (chlorophytes, charophytes), land plants
    - **Unikonta/Amorphea**: Includes amoebozoans (amoebas, slime molds) and opisthokonts (fungi, animals, choanoflagellates)
- **Functional Groups**:
    - **Animal-like (Protozoans)**: Heterotrophs, no cell wall (e.g., amoebas, paramecium, Giardia)
    - **Fungus-like**: Heterotrophs, cell walls, reproduce by spores (e.g., water molds, slime molds)
    - **Plant-like (Algae)**: Autotrophs, cell walls (e.g., diatoms, dinoflagellates, green/red/brown algae)

### Fungi

- Heterotrophs; body structures: multicellular filaments (hyphae forming mycelia) or single cells (yeasts)
- Cell walls made of **chitin**
- **Hyphae Types**: **Septate** (divided by septa), **Coenocytic** (no septa), **Haustoria** (penetrate host tissues)
- **Reproduction**: Sexual (fusion of hyphae from different mating types) and asexual (via spores)
- **Life Cycle**: Includes **plasmogamy** (cytoplasm fusion), **karyogamy** (nuclei fusion), meiosis
- **Major Phyla**:
    - **Chytridiomycota**: Flagellated spores (zoospores)
    - **Zoopagomycetes & Mucoromycetes**: Form zygosporangium (e.g., black bread mold)
    - **Ascomycetes (Sac Fungi)**: Produce spores in asci (e.g., yeast, morels)
    - **Basidiomycetes (Club Fungi)**: Produce spores on basidia (e.g., mushrooms)
- **Mutualisms**:
    - **Mycorrhizae**: Fungal-plant root associations
    - **Lichens**: Fungus + photosynthetic partner (algae/cyanobacterium)

### Plants

- Evolved from charophyte algae; share traits: rose-shaped cellulose synthesis complexes, peroxisome enzymes, flagellated sperm structure, phragmoplast formation
- **Major Groups**:
    - **Nonvascular Plants (Bryophytes)**: Liverworts, mosses, hornworts
    - **Vascular Plants**:
        - **Seedless**: Lycophytes (club mosses), monilophytes (ferns)
        - **Seed Plants**: Gymnosperms (e.g., conifers), angiosperms (flowering plants)

### Animals

- Multicellular, heterotrophic eukaryotes with tissues from embryonic layers
- **Key Features**: Collagen structural proteins; dominant diploid life cycle; embryonic development includes cleavage, blastula, gastrula
- **Embryonic Germ Layers**:
    - **Diploblastic**: Ectoderm and endoderm
    - **Triploblastic**: Ectoderm, endoderm, mesoderm
- **Body Cavities**:
    - **Coelomates**: True coelom from mesoderm (e.g., annelids, chordates)
    - **Pseudocoelomates**: Hemocoel from mesoderm & endoderm (e.g., nematodes)
    - **Acoelomates**: No body cavity (e.g., flatworms)
- **Development**:
    - **Protostomes**: Spiral, determinate cleavage; mouth from blastopore (e.g., mollusks, annelids)
    - **Deuterostomes**: Radial, indeterminate cleavage; anus from blastopore (e.g., echinoderms, chordates)
- **Symmetry**: Radial (cnidarians) or bilateral (most animals); cephalization in bilaterians
- **Phylogeny**: Three major bilaterian lineages:
    - **Deuterostomes**: Echinoderms, hemichordates, chordates
    - **Lophotrochozoans**: Have lophophore or trochophore larva (e.g., flatworms, mollusks, annelids)
    - **Ecdysozoans**: Undergo ecdysis (shed exoskeleton) (e.g., nematodes, arthropods)
- **Invertebrate Phyla**:
    - **Porifera (Sponges)**: No tissues/organs; filter feeders
    - **Cnidaria**: Radial symmetry; gastrovascular cavity; nematocysts (e.g., jellyfish, corals)
    - **Platyhelminthes (Flatworms)**: Acoelomate; cephalization; many parasitic
    - **Mollusca**: Soft-bodied, often shelled; hemocoel (e.g., snails, clams, octopuses)
    - **Annelida (Segmented Worms)**: Coelomate; closed circulatory system (e.g., earthworms)
    - **Nematoda (Roundworms)**: Pseudocoelomate; undergo ecdysis
    - **Arthropoda**: Exoskeleton; segmented with jointed appendages (e.g., insects, spiders, crustaceans)
    - **Echinodermata**: Coelomate; water vascular system; radial symmetry as adults (e.g., starfish)
- **Chordates**: Notochord, dorsal hollow nerve cord, pharyngeal slits, post-anal tail
    - **Vertebrates**: Jawless fish → cartilaginous fish → bony fish → amphibians → reptiles → birds → mammals
- **Vertebrate Classes**:
    - **Fish**: Ectothermic; gills; two-chambered heart (jawless, cartilaginous, bony)
    - **Amphibians**: Ectothermic; metamorphosis; three-chambered heart; moist skin (e.g., frogs)
    - **Reptiles**: Ectothermic; scaly skin; amniotic egg; three- or four-chambered heart
    - **Birds**: Endothermic; feathers; hard-shelled eggs; four-chambered heart
    - **Mammals**: Endothermic; hair/fur; mammary glands; four-chambered heart
        - **Monotremes**: Egg-laying (platypus)
        - **Marsupials**: Pouch-bearing (kangaroo)
        - **Eutherians (Placentals)**: Diverse orders (e.g., primates, rodents, cetartiodactyls)
