# Plant Classification

### **Characteristics of Plants**

- Sessile, multicellular organisms
- Cell walls made of cellulose
- Derived from embryos
- Adapted to land life
- Autotrophic through photosynthesis (contain chlorophyll a and b)
- Closest relatives: modern green algae (charophytes)

### **Derived Traits of Land Plants (Not in Charophytes)**

- **Alternation of Generations**: Life cycle alternates between multicellular haploid (gametophyte) and diploid (sporophyte) stages.
    - Gametophyte (n) produces gametes by mitosis.
    - Sporophyte (2n) produces spores by meiosis.
    - Diploid embryo is retained within female gametophyte tissue.
- **Walled Spores Produced in Sporangia**:
    - Sporocytes (2n) in sporangia undergo meiosis to produce haploid spores.
    - Spore walls contain **sporopollenin** for environmental resistance.
- **Gametes Produced in Gametangia**:
    - **Archegonia**: Female gametangia; produce a single nonmotile egg.
    - **Antheridia**: Male gametangia; produce and release sperm.
    - (Reduced or lost in seed plants)
- **Apical Meristems**: Regions of perpetual cell division at root and shoot tips; enable lifelong growth and tissue differentiation.

### **Evidence for Algal Ancestry**

- Land plants and charophytes share a common ancestor.
- Shared key traits:
    - Rose-shaped complexes for cellulose synthesis
    - Peroxisome enzymes
    - Structure of flagellated sperm
    - Formation of a phragmoplast during cell division

### **Major Plant Clades & Evolutionary Milestones**

1. **Nonvascular Plants (Bryophytes)**: Liverworts, hornworts, mosses.
    - Gametophyte-dominant life cycle.
    - No true vascular tissue.
2. **Vascular Plants (Tracheophytes)**:
    - Have **xylem** and **phloem** for support and transport.
    - Sporophyte-dominant life cycle.
    - **Seedless Vascular Plants**: Lycophytes (club mosses) and Pterophytes (ferns).
    - **Seed Plants**: Gymnosperms and Angiosperms.
3. **Key Adaptations**:
    - Vascular tissue (~420 mya)
    - Seeds (~360 mya)
    - Flowers (in angiosperms)

### **Alternation of Generations Across Plant Groups**

- **Nonvascular Plants**: Dominant gametophyte; small, dependent sporophyte.
- **Vascular Plants**: Dominant, independent sporophyte; reduced gametophytes.
- **Seed Plants**: Gametophytes are microscopic and dependent on sporophyte; heterosporous (produce separate male and female spores).

### **Vascular Tissue: Xylem and Phloem**

- **Xylem**: Conducts water and minerals; contains dead **tracheids** and (in angiosperms) **vessel elements**; strengthened by lignin.
- **Phloem**: Conducts sugars and organic compounds; contains living **sieve-tube elements** and **companion cells**.
- **Vascular Cambium**: Lateral meristem producing secondary xylem (wood) and phloem.

### **Sporophylls and Spore Production**

- **Sporophylls**: Modified leaves bearing sporangia.
    - **Sori**: Clusters of sporangia on fern leaves.
    - **Strobili**: Cone-like structures in lycophytes and gymnosperms.
- **Homosporous**: Produce one spore type → bisexual gametophyte (e.g., most ferns).
- **Heterosporous**: Produce **megaspores** (female) and **microspores** (male) (e.g., all seed plants).

### **Seed Plants: Gymnosperms and Angiosperms**

- **Gymnosperms**: "Naked seeds" exposed on cones.
    - Phyla: Cycadophyta, Ginkgophyta (one species: *Ginkgo biloba*), Gnetophyta, Coniferophyta (largest group).
- **Angiosperms**: Flowering plants; seeds enclosed in ovaries.
    - **Flower Structure**:
        - Sepals (protective)
        - Petals (attract pollinators)
        - Stamens (male: anther + filament)
        - Carpels/Pistils (female: stigma, style, ovary)
    - **Fruit**: Mature ovary; protects and aids seed dispersal.
    - **Placentation**: Ovule attachment within ovary (basal, parietal, axile, free-central, marginal).

### **Flower Development: ABC Model**

- Three classes of floral organ identity genes:
    - **A genes** → Sepals
    - **A + B genes** → Petals
    - **B + C genes** → Stamens
    - **C genes** → Carpels
- **A and C genes are mutually inhibitory**.
- Extended **ABCDE model** includes SEPALLATA (E) and ovule development (D) genes.

### **Monocots vs. Eudicots**

- **Monocots**: One cotyledon; parallel leaf veins; vascular bundles complexly arranged; fibrous roots; floral parts in multiples of three.
- **Eudicots**: Two cotyledons; netlike leaf veins; vascular bundles in a ring; taproot present; floral parts in multiples of four or five.
