Cell structure

Microscopy

Light Microscopy (LM)

  • Uses visible light passed through specimen and glass lenses.
  • Magnification: ~1,000x.
  • Resolution: Can resolve detail finer than ~200 nm.
  • Contrast Enhancement Methods:
    • Brightfield: Light passes directly; little contrast unless stained.
    • Phase-Contrast: Amplifies density variations in unstained, living cells.
    • Differential-Interference-Contrast (DIC): Exaggerates density differences; appears 3D.
    • Fluorescence: Specific molecules labeled with fluorescent dyes/antibodies; absorb UV, emit visible light.
    • Confocal: Uses laser to eliminate out-of-focus light; creates sharp single-plane images; 3D reconstruction possible.

Electron Microscopy (EM)

  • Uses a beam of electrons.
  • Resolution: ~2 nm.
  • Limitation: Cannot study living cells (specimen preparation kills cells).
  • Types:
    • Scanning Electron Microscope (SEM): Studies surface topography; sample coated with gold; produces 3D-like images.
    • Transmission Electron Microscope (TEM): Studies internal structure; beam passes through thin sections; stained with heavy metals.

Basic Cell Components

External Structures

  • Cell wall, capsule, flagellum, cilium, etc.

Core Components

  • Plasma Membrane: Selective barrier.
  • Cytosol: Intracellular semifluid.
  • Chromosome: Stores genetic material; in nucleoid (prokaryotes) or nucleus (eukaryotes).
  • Ribosome: Protein assembly center.

Endomembrane System

  • Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER)
  • Golgi Apparatus
  • Lysosome (phagocytosis & autophagy)
  • Vacuoles

Other Organelles

  • Mitochondrion
  • Peroxisome
  • Plastids (in plants/algae)

Organelle Details

Peroxisome

  • Single-membrane-bound metabolic compartment.
  • Generates H₂O₂ by transferring H atoms to O₂.
  • Functions:
    • Breaks down fatty acids for mitochondria.
    • Detoxifies alcohol (in liver).
  • Contains catalase (marker enzyme) to convert H₂O₂ to water.
  • Glyoxysome: Specialized peroxisome in plant seeds; converts fatty acids to sugar for seedlings.

Plastids (Plant/Algal Cells)

  • Membrane-bound; own DNA; replicate independently.
  • Leucoplast (non-pigmented):
    • Amyloplast: Starch synthesis.
    • Elaioplast: Lipid synthesis.
    • Proteinoplast: Protein synthesis.
  • Chromoplast: Pigment synthesis/storage.
  • Etioplast: Develops in darkness; can become chloroplast.
  • Gerontoplast: From chloroplast during senescence; loses thylakoids.

Protein Targeting to ER

  1. Polypeptide synthesis begins on free ribosome.
  2. SRP (Signal Recognition Particle) binds signal peptide, halting synthesis.
  3. SRP binds receptor in ER membrane (forms pore).
  4. SRP leaves; synthesis resumes with translocation across membrane.
  5. Signal peptide cleaved.
  6. Polypeptide folds into final conformation.

Cytoskeleton

Overview

  • Network of fibers organizing cell structure/activities.
  • Components: Microtubules, Microfilaments, Intermediate Filaments.

Microtubules

  • Structure: Hollow tube; wall of 13 protofilaments of α/β-tubulin dimers.
  • Diameter: 25 nm.
  • Polarity: Plus (+) and minus (–) ends.
  • Nucleotide: GTP.
  • Functions:
    • Cytosolic: Cell shape, polarity, chromosome movement, organelle transport.
    • Axonemal: Cell motility (cilia/flagella).
  • Dynamic Instability: Grow/shrink based on GTP-tubulin concentration.

Microfilaments (Actin Filaments)

  • Structure: Two intertwined chains of F-actin.
  • Diameter: 7 nm.
  • Monomers: G-actin (globular).
  • Polarity: Plus (+) and minus (–) ends.
  • Nucleotide: ATP.
  • Functions: Muscle contraction, cell locomotion, cytoplasmic streaming, cytokinesis, cell shape, intracellular transport.

Intermediate Filaments

  • Structure: Eight protofilaments joined end-to-end with staggered overlaps.
  • Diameter: 8-12 nm.
  • Polarity: None.
  • Nucleotide: None.
  • Functions: Structural support, cell shape, nuclear lamina, axon strength (neurofilaments), muscle fiber alignment (desmin).
  • Classes:
    • I/II: Cytokeratins (epithelial cells; mechanical strength).
    • III: Vimentin (fibroblasts), Desmin (muscle), GFA protein (glial cells).
    • IV: Neurofilament proteins (nerves; axon strength/size).
    • V: Nuclear lamins (all eukaryotes; nuclear scaffold).
    • VI: Nestin (neuronal stem cells).

Cytoskeletal Motors & Motility

Microtubule-Based Transport

  • Motor Proteins:
    • Kinesins: Move toward (+) end (outbound/periphery).
    • Dynein: Moves toward (–) end (inbound/center).
      • Cytoplasmic dynein: Vesicle/organelle transport.
      • Axonemal dynein: Powers cilia/flagella beating.

Cilia & Flagella

  • Cilia: Short (2-10 μm); many per cell; beat parallel to surface.
  • Flagella: Long (1 μm to mm); undulatory motion; moves cell along axis.
  • Structure:
    • Basal Body: Anchors; similar to centriole (9+0 microtubule triplets).
    • Axoneme: Core with 9 outer microtubule doublets + 2 central singlet microtubules (9+2 pattern).
      • Dynein arms connect doublets; ATP hydrolysis causes sliding → bending.
      • Nexin links & radial spokes coordinate sliding into bending.
    • Primary Cilia: Sensory; 9+0 pattern (no central pair).

Microfilament-Based Motors (Myosins)

  • Superfamily of actin-based motors using ATP.
  • Functions: Endocytosis, muscle contraction, cytokinesis.

Muscle Contraction

Sarcomere Structure

  • Sarcomere: Basic contractile unit of myofibril.
  • Bands:
    • A band: Dark; contains thick filaments (myosin).
    • I band: Light; contains thin filaments (actin).
    • H zone: Center of A band (only thick filaments).
    • Z line: Middle of I band; defines sarcomere boundaries.
    • M line: Center; links myosin filaments.

Filaments

  • Thick Filaments: Myosin molecules in staggered array; heads form cross-bridges.
  • Thin Filaments: F-actin, tropomyosin, troponin complex (TnT, TnC, TnI).
    • TnT binds tropomyosin.
    • TnC binds Ca²⁺.
    • TnI inhibits contraction by binding actin.

Sliding-Filament Model

  • Muscle contraction = thin filaments slide past thick filaments; filament lengths don't change.
  • Regulation by Ca²⁺:
    • Low Ca²⁺: Tropomyosin blocks myosin-binding sites on actin.
    • High Ca²⁺: Ca²⁺ binds TnC → conformational change → myosin binds actin → contraction.

Extracellular Structures

Plant Cell Wall

  • Primary Cell Wall: Thin, flexible; cellulose, hemicellulose, pectins, extensins.
  • Middle Lamella: Pectin-rich layer between adjacent primary walls.
  • Secondary Cell Wall: Thick, strong; cellulose + lignin; deposited inside primary wall.

Animal Extracellular Matrix (ECM)

  • Components:
    • Fibers: Collagen (strength), Elastin (elasticity).
    • Matrix: Proteoglycans (hydrated gel).
    • Adhesive Glycoproteins: Fibronectin (binds cells to ECM), Laminin.
  • Integrins: Transmembrane receptors link intracellular microfilaments to ECM proteins.
  • Examples: Bone (rigid), Cartilage (mostly matrix), Connective tissue (gel-like).

Cell Junctions

Plant Cells: Plasmodesmata

  • Membrane-lined channels connecting cytoplasm of adjacent cells.
  • Allow passage of water, solutes, proteins, RNA.

Animal Cells

  • Anchoring Junctions (Desmosomes):
    • Connect cells via proteins linked to intermediate filaments (keratin).
    • Provide mechanical strength (e.g., epithelial tissues).
  • Tight Junctions:
    • Seal adjacent cell membranes; prevent fluid leakage.
    • Restrict lateral movement of membrane lipids/proteins.
  • Gap Junctions:
    • Formed by connexins (form connexon channels).
    • Allow direct cytoplasmic communication (ions, small molecules).
    • Important in heart muscle, neurons, embryos.
