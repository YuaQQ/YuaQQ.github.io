# Angiosperm Reproduction

### **Angiosperm Life Cycle**

- Dominant **sporophyte (2n)** generation.
- **Male Gametophyte (Pollen Grain)**:
    - Develops from microspore in anther.
    - Contains generative cell (→ 2 sperm) and tube cell (→ pollen tube).
- **Female Gametophyte (Embryo Sac)**:
    - Develops from megaspore in ovule.
    - Contains: 1 egg cell, 2 synergids, 3 antipodal cells, 1 central cell with 2 polar nuclei.

### **Pollination and Double Fertilization**

- **Pollination**: Transfer of pollen from anther to stigma.
- **Pollen Tube** grows through style to ovary.
- **Double Fertilization**:
    1. **Sperm + Egg → Zygote (2n) → Embryo**.
    2. **Sperm + 2 Polar Nuclei → Endosperm (3n)** (nutritive tissue).

### **Seed Development**

- **Ovule → Seed**:
    - **Integument → Seed Coat**.
    - **Zygote → Embryo**.
    - **Triploid Cell → Endosperm**.
- **Seed Structure**:
    - **Eudicot (e.g., bean)**: Two cotyledons; embryonic axis (hypocotyl, radicle, epicotyl).
    - **Monocot (e.g., corn)**: One cotyledon (scutellum); **coleoptile** (shoot sheath) and **coleorhiza** (root sheath).

### **Seed Germination**

- **Dormancy Break**: Requires environmental cues (temperature, light, water).
- **Imbibition**: Water uptake initiates metabolic activity.
- **Germination Process**:
    - Radicle emerges first.
    - Shoot emerges (hypocotyl arch in eudicots; coleoptile in monocots).
    - Cotyledons may photosynthesize or wither.
