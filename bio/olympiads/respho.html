Respiration & Photosynthesis

Cellular Respiration: Harvesting Chemical Energy

Three Main Stages

  • Glycolysis: Breaks down glucose into two molecules of pyruvate; occurs in the cytosol.
  • Citric Acid Cycle (Krebs/TCA cycle): Completes the oxidation of glucose; occurs in the mitochondrial matrix.
  • Oxidative Phosphorylation: Electron transport chain and chemiosmosis; accounts for most ATP synthesis; occurs in the inner mitochondrial membrane.

Glycolysis

  • Location: Cytosol.
  • Two Phases:
    • Energy Investment Phase: Consumes 2 ATP.
    • Energy Payoff Phase: Produces 4 ATP and 2 NADH.
  • Net Gain per Glucose: 2 ATP, 2 NADH, 2 pyruvate.
  • Key Enzyme: Phosphofructokinase (regulatory step).

Citric Acid Cycle

  • Location: Mitochondrial matrix.
  • Input per Pyruvate: Acetyl-CoA (from pyruvate oxidation).
  • Output per Turn: 3 NADH, 1 FADH₂, 1 ATP (or GTP), 2 CO₂.
  • Key Features:
    • Discovered by Hans Krebs & Fritz Lipman.
    • Two phases: carbon oxidation (steps 1-4) and regeneration of oxaloacetate (steps 5-8).
    • In animals, step 5 produces GTP; in bacteria/plants, produces ATP.

Oxidative Phosphorylation

  • Electron Transport Chain (ETC):
    • Located in the inner mitochondrial membrane (cristae).
    • Electrons from NADH and FADH₂ are passed through protein complexes (I-IV) to O₂, forming H₂O.
    • ETC does not make ATP directly; it creates a proton gradient.
  • Chemiosmosis:
    • Proton gradient (proton-motive force) drives ATP synthesis via ATP synthase (Complex V).
    • ATP synthase (F₀F₁ complex) uses proton flow to phosphorylate ADP → ATP.
    • Approx. 3 protons needed per ATP.
    • Proton pumps: NADH → 10 H⁺ pumped; FADH₂ → 6 H⁺ pumped.
  • Electron Shuttles (move cytosolic NADH into mitochondria):
    • Glycerol-Phosphate Shuttle: Used in muscle/brain; yields FADH₂.
    • Malate-Aspartate Shuttle: Used in heart/liver/kidney; yields NADH.

ATP Yield per Glucose (Aerobic)

  • Glycolysis: 2 ATP (net) + 2 NADH (→ 3-5 ATP via shuttles).
  • Pyruvate → Acetyl-CoA: 2 NADH (→ 5 ATP).
  • Citric Acid Cycle: 2 ATP + 6 NADH (→ 15 ATP) + 2 FADH₂ (→ 3 ATP).
  • Total Maximum: ~36-38 ATP.

Anaerobic Pathways

  • Fermentation: Glycolysis + NAD⁺ regeneration; no O₂ required.
    • Lactic Acid Fermentation: Pyruvate → lactate; regenerates NAD⁺.
    • Alcohol Fermentation: Pyruvate → ethanol + CO₂; regenerates NAD⁺.
  • Organism Types:
    • Obligate Anaerobes: Cannot survive in O₂.
    • Facultative Anaerobes (e.g., yeast): Can use fermentation or respiration.

Metabolic Integration

  • Carbohydrates: Enter as sugars → glycolysis.
  • Fats: Glycerol → glycolysis; fatty acids → β-oxidation → acetyl-CoA.
  • Proteins: Amino acids → pyruvate, acetyl-CoA, or TCA intermediates.
  • Fat Yield: >2x ATP per gram vs. carbohydrates.

Photosynthesis: Capturing Light Energy

Overview

  • Photoautotrophs: Use light energy to fix CO₂ into organic molecules (in chloroplasts).
  • Two Sets of Reactions:
    • Light-Dependent Reactions: In thylakoid membranes; produce ATP & NADPH.
    • Light-Independent Reactions (Calvin Cycle): In stroma; use ATP & NADPH to fix CO₂.

Light-Dependent Reactions

  • Photosystems: Protein-pigment complexes (PSII & PSI).
  • Photosystem II (P680):
    • Absorbs light; oxidizes water → O₂ + H⁺ + e⁻ (photolysis).
    • Electrons pass to plastoquinone (PQ) → cytochrome b6/f complex → plastocyanin (PC).
  • Photosystem I (P700):
    • Re-excites electrons from PC; passes to ferredoxin (Fd) → NADP⁺ → NADPH (via FNR).
  • Linear Electron Flow: Produces both ATP and NADPH (requires both photosystems).
  • Cyclic Electron Flow: Only PSI; electrons cycle back to cytochrome b6/f; produces ATP only (no NADPH or O₂).
  • ATP Synthesis (Photophosphorylation):
    • Proton gradient across thylakoid membrane drives CF₀CF₁ ATP synthase.
    • Approx. 4 H⁺ per ATP.

Light-Independent Reactions (Calvin Cycle)

  • Location: Stroma.
  • Three Phases:
    1. Carbon Fixation: RuBisCO fixes CO₂ to RuBP → 3PGA.
    2. Reduction: 3PGA uses ATP & NADPH → G3P (sugar precursor).
    3. Regeneration: Most G3P regenerates RuBP using ATP.
  • Net Output per 3 CO₂: 1 G3P (for sugar synthesis).

Photosynthetic Pigments

  • Chlorophyll a: Primary pigment.
  • Chlorophyll b, c, d: Accessory pigments in various algae/plants.
  • Carotenoids (β-carotene, lutein): Absorb blue-green light; photoprotection.
  • Phycobilins (phycoerythrin, phycocyanin): In red algae & cyanobacteria.

Photorespiration & Adaptations

  • Photorespiration: RuBisCO binds O₂ instead of CO₂; wasteful; occurs under hot/dry/ bright conditions.
  • Plant Adaptations:
    • C₃ Plants: Basic Calvin cycle; photorespiration occurs.
    • C₄ Plants (e.g., corn):
      • Spatial separation: CO₂ fixed into oxaloacetate (via PEP carboxylase) in mesophyll → malate → bundle sheath cells → release CO₂ for Calvin cycle.
      • Minimizes photorespiration.
    • CAM Plants (e.g., cacti):
      • Temporal separation: Stomata open at night; fix CO₂ into malate (stored in vacuole). Day: malate decarboxylated → CO₂ for Calvin cycle.
      • Reduces water loss.

Regulation & Photoinhibition

  • Calvin Cycle Activation: Light increases stromal pH & Mg²⁺; activates enzymes (e.g., RuBisCO).
  • Photoinhibition: Excess light damages PSII (D1 protein) via reactive oxygen species (ROS).
