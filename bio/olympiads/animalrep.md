# Animal Reproduction

## Male Reproductive System

### Anatomy & Functions

- **Testes** (in scrotum): produce sperm (spermatogenesis) and testosterone.
- **Epididymis**: sperm maturation and storage.
- **Vas deferens**: propels sperm from epididymis to urethra.
- **Seminal vesicles**: produce ~60% of semen fluid; contains fructose (energy) and prostaglandins (stimulate uterine contractions).
- **Prostate gland**: secretes alkaline fluid with anticoagulant enzymes and citrate; neutralizes vaginal acidity.
- **Bulbourethral glands**: secrete clear mucus to lubricate urethra and neutralize residual urine.
- **Urethra**: passage for semen and urine.
- **Penis**: delivers sperm into female reproductive tract via erection and ejaculation.

### Spermatogenesis

- Occurs in **seminiferous tubules** of testes.
- **Spermatogonia** (diploid stem cells) undergo mitosis to maintain population and meiosis to produce sperm.
- **Primary spermatocytes** (2n) → Meiosis I → **secondary spermatocytes** (n) → Meiosis II → **spermatids** (n) → **spermiogenesis** → **spermatozoa** (mature sperm).
- **Sertoli cells**: nourish developing sperm; form blood‑testis barrier.
- **Leydig cells** (interstitial cells): produce testosterone.
- **Mature sperm structure**:
    - **Head**: contains nucleus and acrosome (enzymes to penetrate egg).
    - **Midpiece**: packed with mitochondria for ATP.
    - **Tail** (flagellum): propulsion.

### Hormonal Regulation in Males

- **FSH**: stimulates Sertoli cells to support spermatogenesis.
- **LH**: stimulates Leydig cells to produce testosterone.
- **Testosterone**: promotes sperm maturation, male secondary sex characteristics, muscle/bone growth.
- Negative feedback: testosterone inhibits GnRH, FSH, and LH secretion.

## Female Reproductive System

### Anatomy & Functions

- **Ovaries**: produce oocytes (eggs), estrogen, and progesterone.
- **Oviducts (fallopian tubes)**: site of fertilization; transport egg to uterus.
- **Uterus**: site of embryo implantation and fetal development.
- **Endometrium**: inner lining of uterus; thickens during menstrual cycle.
- **Cervix**: lower part of uterus; opens into vagina.
- **Vagina**: receives sperm; birth canal.
- **External genitalia**: labia, clitoris, Bartholin’s glands (lubrication).

### Oogenesis

- Begins during fetal development; primary oocytes arrested in prophase I until puberty.
- Each menstrual cycle, a few **primary follicles** develop.
- **Primary oocyte** (2n) completes Meiosis I → **secondary oocyte** (n) + first polar body (degenerates).
- Secondary oocyte arrested in metaphase II until fertilization.
- After fertilization, Meiosis II completes → **ovum** (n) + second polar body.
- **Follicle cells** surround oocyte; secrete estrogen.
- **Ovulation**: release of secondary oocyte from mature (Graafian) follicle.
- **Corpus luteum**: forms from ruptured follicle; secretes progesterone and estrogen.

### Menstrual Cycle (~28 days)

- **Ovarian cycle**:
    - **Follicular phase** (days 1‑14): FSH stimulates follicle growth; rising estrogen.
    - **Ovulation** (day ~14): LH surge triggers release of secondary oocyte.
    - **Luteal phase** (days 15‑28): corpus luteum secretes progesterone and estrogen.
- **Uterine cycle**:
    - **Menstrual phase** (days 1‑5): shedding of endometrium.
    - **Proliferative phase** (days 6‑14): estrogen stimulates endometrial thickening.
    - **Secretory phase** (days 15‑28): progesterone maintains endometrium for possible implantation.

### Hormonal Regulation in Females

- **FSH**: stimulates follicle growth and estrogen secretion.
- **LH**: triggers ovulation; stimulates corpus luteum formation.
- **Estrogen** (from growing follicles): thickens endometrium; develops female secondary sex characteristics; positive feedback on LH/FSH surge.
- **Progesterone** (from corpus luteum): maintains endometrium; inhibits FSH/LH (negative feedback).
- **hCG** (human chorionic gonadotropin): secreted by embryo after implantation; maintains corpus luteum during early pregnancy.

## Fertilization & Early Development

### Fertilization Process

1. **Sperm attraction**: chemotaxis toward egg in oviduct.
2. **Acrosome reaction**: sperm releases hydrolytic enzymes to penetrate zona pellucida.
3. **Sperm‑egg fusion**: plasma membranes fuse; sperm nucleus enters egg.
4. **Cortical reaction**: cortical granules release enzymes that harden zona pellucida (block polyspermy).
5. **Completion of meiosis II** in secondary oocyte → ovum.

### Early Embryonic Development

- **Cleavage**: rapid mitotic divisions without growth → **morula** (solid ball of cells).
- **Blastocyst formation**: fluid‑filled cavity (blastocoel) forms; cells differentiate into:
    - **Trophoblast**: outer layer; implants into endometrium; forms placenta.
    - **Inner cell mass**: becomes embryo.
        - Inner cell mass → 3 germ layers by gastrulation
        - Ectoderm
            - Nervous system
            - lens
            - epidermis of skin
        - Mesoderm
            - bones
            - skeletal muscle
            - dermis of skin
            - kidney
            - gonads
            - heart & blood vessels
        - Endoderm
            - Respiratory tract
            - Digestive tract
            - Liver, pancreas
            - Urinary bladder & urethra
            - Thyroid gland, parathyroid gland, thymus
- **Implantation**: blastocyst embeds in endometrium (~7 days after fertilization).

### Placenta & Pregnancy

- **Placenta**: formed from trophoblast and maternal tissue; site of nutrient/gas/waste exchange.
- Secretes **hCG** (maintains corpus luteum), **estrogen**, and **progesterone**.
- **Amniotic sac & fluid**: cushion and protect fetus.
- **Umbilical cord**: connects fetus to placenta (two arteries, one vein).

### Birth (Parturition)

1. **Progesterone drop** allows uterine contractions.
2. **Prostaglandins** and **oxytocin** stimulate contractions (positive feedback).
3. **Cervical dilation** → expulsion of baby → delivery of placenta.

## Sex Hormones Overview

- **Androgens** (e.g., testosterone): male development, spermatogenesis.
- **Estrogens** (e.g., estradiol): female development, endometrial growth.
- **Progestins** (e.g., progesterone): maintain endometrium, pregnancy.
- All are steroid hormones derived from cholesterol.

## Common Sexually Transmitted Diseases (STDs)

- **Syphilis** (*Treponema pallidum* bacterium): painless sore → rash → CNS damage if untreated; treated with antibiotics.
- **Gonorrhea** (*Neisseria gonorrhoeae* bacterium): can cause urethritis, pelvic inflammatory disease; treated with antibiotics.
- **Trichomoniasis** (*Trichomonas vaginalis* protozoan): vaginitis/urethritis; treated with antibiotics.
- **HPV** (human papillomavirus): causes genital warts and cancers (cervical, anal, etc.); preventable by vaccine.
