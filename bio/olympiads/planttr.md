# Plant Nutrient and Transport

### **Mineral Acquisition by Roots**

- Roots absorb water and minerals from soil.
- **Mycorrhizae**: Symbiotic associations with fungal hyphae; increase absorption surface area.

### **Transport Pathways in Roots**

- **Apoplastic Route**: Through cell walls and extracellular spaces.
- **Symplastic Route**: Through cytoplasm via plasmodesmata.
- **Transmembrane Route**: Across cell membranes and walls.
- **Endodermis & Casparian Strip**: Final checkpoint; forces water/minerals to enter symplast for selective entry into stele.

### **Long-Distance Transport: Bulk Flow**

- Movement driven by pressure gradients.
- **Xylem Transport**: Driven by negative pressure (transpiration pull).
- **Phloem Transport**: Driven by positive pressure (pressure flow).

### **Xylem Transport Mechanisms**

- **Root Pressure**: Minor push from root cortex (causes **guttation** in small plants).
- **Transpiration-Cohesion-Tension Theory**:
    1. **Transpiration**: Water evaporates from leaf stomata.
    2. **Tension**: Negative pressure pulls water column upward.
    3. **Cohesion**: Hydrogen bonding between water molecules.
    4. **Adhesion**: Water bonds to hydrophilic xylem walls.

### **Stomatal Regulation**

- **Guard Cells** control stomatal aperture via turgor pressure changes.
- **Opening Mechanism**:
    1. Proton pumps export H⁺.
    2. Voltage drives K⁺ influx.
    3. Water enters by osmosis → guard cells become turgid.
- **Closing Mechanism**: K⁺ efflux → water leaves → guard cells become flaccid.
- **Stimuli for Opening**: Light, low CO₂, circadian rhythm.
- **Stimuli for Closing**: Water deficiency (via **abscisic acid, ABA**), drought, high temperature, wind.

### **Plant Adaptations to Extreme Environments**

- **Halophytes** (high salinity): Shrunken stomata, long roots, salt-secreting structures.
- **Xerophytes** (dry habitats): Reduced leaf area, thick cuticles, night-opening stomata, water-storing tissues.

### **Phloem Transport: Translocation**

- **Phloem Sap**: Aqueous solution high in sucrose.
- **Source-to-Sink Flow**:
    - **Source**: Organ producing/storing sugars (e.g., leaves).
    - **Sink**: Organ consuming/storing sugars (e.g., roots, fruits).
- **Pressure Flow Model**:
    1. **Loading at Source**: Sucrose actively transported into sieve tubes (via proton cotransport).
    2. **Water Entry**: Lowers water potential; water enters from xylem by osmosis → increases turgor pressure.
    3. **Flow to Sink**: Pressure gradient pushes sap toward sink.
    4. **Unloading at Sink**: Sucrose removed; water exits back to xylem.

### **Plant Nutrition: Essential Elements**

- **Macronutrients** (required in large amounts):
    - C, O, H, N, P, S, K, Ca, Si, Mg.
    - Functions: Component of macromolecules, enzymes, chlorophyll, structural support.
- **Micronutrients** (required in trace amounts):
    - Na, Cl, Fe, Mn, B, Zn, Cu, Ni, Mo.
    - Functions: Enzyme cofactors, redox reactions, specialized metabolism.
- **Deficiency Symptoms**:
    - **Nitrogen (N)**: Chlorosis (yellowing) in older leaves; stunted growth.
    - **Phosphorus (P)**: Stunted growth; purple leaves (anthocyanin accumulation).
    - **Potassium (K)**: Marginal chlorosis/necrosis in older leaves.
    - **Magnesium (Mg)**: Interveinal chlorosis in older leaves.

### **Nitrogen Fixation**

- **Rhizobium** bacteria in legume root nodules fix atmospheric N₂.
- **Leghemoglobin** in nodules: "Oxygen buffer" maintains anaerobic conditions for nitrogenase activity.
