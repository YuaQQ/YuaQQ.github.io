一、FRQ构成

1. Interpreting and Evaluating Experimental Results (8-10pt).

Part A (2pt): Describe and explain biological concepts, processes, or models.

  • Describe – 1pt; Explain – 1pt; using essential knowledge.

Part B (3-4pt. Usually 3pt according to the samples): Identify experimental design procedures.

  • Identify independent variable/dependent variable/control. May be variable + control identification = 2pt
    • Check 1.1 for variable identification
    • Check 1.2 for control identification
  • Justify the use of xxx as control. AND/OR Justify the use of the data range/multiple data points/introduction of one independent variable in a single attempt. (1-2pt)
    • Check 2.1 for justifying control
    • Check 2.2 for justifying usage of a range of independent variables

Part C (1-3pt): Analyze data.

  • Based on the data, describe the effect of A on B / Describe the relationship between A and B (1pt)
    • Check 4.3

OR

  • Identify the experimental setting (of independent variables) that meets a certain requirement (for the dependent variable). (1pt)
    • Check 4.2

OR

  • Calculate/Determine a certain quantity (e.g. ratio, percent change, etc.) (1pt)
    • Check 4.1

Part D (2-4pt): Make and justify predictions.

*Note: Sometimes Part D may ask you to state a null hypothesis of the experiment

  • Check 1.3 for null hypothesis

  • Provide explanation with reference to essential knowledge.

二、常见题型

1. Identify experimental design procedures.

FRQ 1b, 3b for independent variable/dependent variable/control identification;

Sometimes FRQ 1d, 3c for null hypothesis statement.

1.1 Identify an independent variable / a dependent variable (based on Table/Figure).

FRQ 1b, 3b.

答题思路：

Independent variable is the one manipulated in treatment and left untouched in negative control. Dependent variable is the one measured.

Check the corresponding Table or Figure. Independent variable (that is actually manipulated) is often found on the x-axis, while the dependent variable (that is actually measured; sometimes used to represent the conceptual dependent variable raised in the scientific question) is often found on the y-axis.

Find the hypothesis/scientific question/experimental setup in the question stem.

E.g.

a. “Scientists treated normal human kidney cells and PKD cells with increasing concentrations of ouabain and measured the number of cells and the activity of Na+/K+ ATPase.” (2021-1b)

b. “To study the role of different proteins in the PHO pathway, researchers used a wild-type strain of yeast to create a strain with a mutant form of Pho81 and a strain with a mutated form of Pho4. …they measured the activity of APase.” (2023-1b)

c. “Scientists studied the effects of cholera toxin on four samples … GTP was added to samples II and IV only; cholera toxin was added to samples III and IV only. The scientists then measured the amount of cAMP produced…” (2022-1b)

Back

1.2 Identify a (negative) control in the experiment.

FRQ 1b, 3b.

答题方式：

The sample lacking the independent variable (the underlined part needs to be changed to adapt to the question scenario).

OR

The sample with the independent variable left untouched.

E.g.

a. The sample lacking cholera toxin. (2022-1b)

b. “Identify the difference between the control cells and the experimental cells used in the experiment.” (2020 practice 3-1b)

  • The control cells were the same type of cells, infected with the parasite, but not treated with any chemicals, as were the experimental cells.

Back

1.3 State a null hypothesis for the experiment.

FRQ 3c (sometimes) 1d (sometimes).

答题方式：

The independent variable has no effect on the dependent variable (the underlined part needs to be changed to adapt to the question scenario).

E.g.

a. The modified RNA polymerase would not affect the maximum elongation rate. (2020 practice 1-1d)

b. The color of the enclosure ground will not affect the frequency of light and dark mice in the population. (2020 practice 3-3c)

c. Temperature has no effect on the amount of light emitted. (2022-3c)

d. Climate change/Temperature/CO2 levels will have no effect on sand lance embryo survival rate. (2023-3c)

Back

2. Justify an experimental design

2.1 Justify the use of xxx as control in the experiment.

FRQ 1b (sometimes), 3b (sometimes).

答题方式：

(Provide reasoning in complete sentence.) It allows the attribution of the change in dependent variable measured to the independent variable.

OR

It serves as the basis for comparison of the effect on dependent variable by the independent variable.

E.g.

a. “Justify the use of normal human kidney cells as a control in the experiments.” (2021-1b)

  • It allows the scientists to determine the effect of PKD on cells’ responses to ouabin. (PKD: independent variable; ouabin: dependent variable)

b. “Justify why the scientists included Sample III (GTP(-), cholera toxin(+)) as a control treatment in the experiment.” (2022-1b)

  • It (comparing sample III with sample IV (GTP(+), cholera toxin(+))) enables the scientists to evaluate whether cAMP production under the activity of cholera toxin requires GTP. (presence of GTP: independent variable; rate of cAMP production: dependent variable)

c. “Justify the researcher’s using the wild-type strain for the creation of mutant strains.” (2023-1b)

  • It ensures any observed differences in APase activity/relative amounts of PHO1 mRNA between the strains are due to the introduced mutations. (certain type of mutation: independent variable; APase activity/relative amounts of PHO1 mRNA: dependent variable)

d. "Justify the decision of the researchers to hold lighting conditions constant in their experiment." (2020 practice 1-3b)

  • Since pigeons clean their feathers by sight, variable light conditions would affect the effectiveness of this behavior. (color contrast between the lice and the pigeon: independent variable; effectiveness of pigeon cleaning its feather: dependent behavior)

Back

2.2 Justify the use of a range of levels (e.g. time points, concentrations) of the independent variable

FRQ 1b (iii) (sometimes)

答题方式：

(Provide reasoning in complete sentence.) It monitors an accurate trend of changes in dependent variable (to determine whether different levels of independent variable have different effects on dependent variable / to determine at which level does the independent variable start to have a significant effect on the dependent variable). (the underlined part needs to be changed to adapt to the question scenario)

E.g.

a. “Justify the researcher analyzing blood samples at many intermediate time points instead of at only the beginning and the end of the 20-week period.” (2020 practice 2 - FRQ1 b(iii))

  • By collecting more data, the researcher will see a more accurate trend.

b. “Justify the researchers using a different range of concentrations for FX11 than was used for gossypol.” (2020 practice 3 - FRQ1 b(iii))

  • FX11 had minimal impact on the growth of C. parvum until it reached a concentration of greater than 30μM, which was the maximum concentration of gossypol that was tested. (concentration of FX11 / gossypol: independent variable; growth of C.parvum: dependent variable)

c. “Justify the use of a range of ouabain concentrations in the experiment represented in Figure 1.” (2021 FRQ1 b(iii))

  • The scientists need to determine whether different concentrations have different effects on the cell numbers.
  • The scientists did not know at which concentration of ouabain there would be an effect.

(concentrations of ouabain: independent variable; cell number: dependent variable)

Back

3. Construct a graph, plot or chart and use confidence intervals or error bars.

3.1 Use the template provided to construct an appropriately labeled graph based on the data in the Table.

FRQ 2b(i).

答题要点（从中选出三点作为得分点）:

  • Appropriate scaling
  • Axis labels (origin, arrows, variable names (and units in brackets))
  • Graph type
    • Bar graph (grouped bar graph with legend if ≥ 2 categorical independent variables)
    • Line graph (multiple lines with legend if ≥ 2 variables; one quantitative, and the other(s) categorical)
    • Scatter plot
  • Error bars
  • Data points.

建议步骤：

a. Use a ruler (or substitutes like a card) and a pen, draw the x and y axes first, with addition of arrows on the ends of both axes. Label the origin.

b. Label the names of IV and DV on x and y axes, respectively. If the variables have units, include the units in brackets. Do not include .

c. Determine the range of scales on the x & y axes by observing the range of data listed (remember to calculate the upper and lower limits determined by the standard errors, if given). The axis should be scaled in equally segmented manner with each segment representing a defined range. If the starting point of a data set is far from zero and the data are clustered (e.g. 2000, 2004, 2014, 2035), a segment of zigzag polyline may be placed at the bottom of the corresponding axis to represent a “folding” of the range (e.g. below 2000) that is not needed. i.e. to construct a truncated axis

d. Plot the data points and error bars.

e. Determine whether bar graph/line graph should be plotted (Bar graph – applied when IV is categorical and DV is quantitative; Line graph – applied when IV is quantitative and DV is quantitative and a trend in the change of DV is assumed as IV is gradually increased/decreased; in AP biology, use short lines to connect the neighboring scatter points to represent a general trend).

Back

3.2 Determine which xxx varies (vary) significantly different from xxx. / Determine whether xxx differs from xxx.

FRQ 2b (ii)

答题思路：

If two samples give their 95% confidence intervals () with no overlap (i.e. no overlap between the range defined by their error bars), this means that there is no way that their assumed population means are equal, so we may say that “the DV in sample 1 is significantly higher/lower than the DV in sample 2”.

If two samples give their 95% confidence intervals () with some overlap (i.e. overlap between the range defined by their error bars), this means that their assumed population means may be equal, so we may say that “the DV in sample 1 is not significantly different from the DV in sample 2”.

无需完整句子。

E.g.

a. “Determine whether there is a statistical difference in the amount of CO2 fixed by rubisco at 30°C and 40°C.” (2023 practice 1-2b)

b. “Based on the data, determine the concentration(s) of paclitaxel that is (are) most effective in causing tripolar cell division.” (2023 practice 3-2b)

c. “Determine one individual who is both at risk of developing the disorder and has a significantly different blood glucose level from that of individual IV-1.” (2021-2b)

d. “Based on the data, determine whether corn strains I, II, and III differ in their average number of crossovers.” (2022-2b)

Back

4. Analyze data.

FRQ 1c, 2c, 6a, 6b

4.1. Calculate xxx / Calculate the percent change in xxx.

FRQ 1c (Sometimes).

答题要点：

Perform mathematical steps with units, followed by giving an answer with the required rounded decimals with unit. If “percent” is required, write “×100%” in the steps and the answer must be in %.

Be careful with “Calculate the percent change in A compared to B”. The answer is usually calculated by (A-B)/B×100%

E.g.

a. “Calculate the expected Na+/K+ ATPase activity (unit/sec) in PKD cells treated with 106 pM oubain.” (2021-1c)

b. “Calculate the percent change in the rate of cAMP production due to the presence of cholera toxin in sample IV compared with sample II.” (2022-1c)

c. “Calculate the percent change in APase activity in wild-type yeast cells in a high-Pi environment compared with that of wild-type cells in a low-Pi environment.” (2023-1c)

Back

4.2 Based on the data, identify the experimental settings (of the independent variable) that meets certain requirement (for the dependent variable).

FRQ 1c (Sometimes), 2c (Sometimes), 6a, 6b (Sometimes).

答题思路：

Check the corresponding table or graph for the data point(s) that meet the requirements for the dependent variable.

无需完整句子

E.g.

a. “Identify the temperature at which the soybean leaves are producing the lowest amount of carbohydrate.” (2020 practice 1-2c)

  • Any value above 45°C and equal to or below 50°C

b. “Based on the data, identify the lowest level of paclitaxel that will allow for at least 50% of the cells to be tripolar.” (2020 practice 3-2c)

  • 4 nM

c. “Based on the data in Table 1, identify the yeast strain and growth conditions that lead to the highest relative amount of PHO1 mRNA.” (2023-1c)

  • Wild-type yeast in a low-Pi environment.

d. “Based on the pedigree, identify all individuals in generation IV who can pass on the mutation to their children.” (2021-2c)

  • IV-1, IV-2, IV-4

Back

4.3 Based on the data, describe the relationship between variable A and variable B. / the effect of independent variable on dependent variable.

FRQ 1c (Sometimes), 2c (Sometimes), 6b (Sometimes)

答题方式：

The independent variable increases / decreases the dependent variable (within a specific range).

*Note:

  • Usually used in answers to questions structured like “describe the effect of A on B”. Cannot be applied in observational study situations where correlation rather than causation can be concluded from the data. (Causation can be concluded ONLY in experiments, where researchers manipulate independent variables to find whether a change in the dependent variable is caused; also, observational studies cannot maintain other variables the same between the control and the treatment groups).
  • A (significant) increase or decrease may be concluded for the two data points compared provided that the confidence intervals defined by their error bars DO NOT overlap; if the confidence intervals overlap, there is no significant difference between them.
  • If the trend in different intervals in the defined range of the data is different, describe the sub-trends in piecewise manner.

OR

There is a positive / negative correlation between variable A and variable B.

*Note:

  • Usually used in answers to question structured like “describe the relationship between A and B.”
  • Require at least 3 data points that have a linear correlation to make the statement. The error bars defining the confidence intervals should not overlap.
  • If the trend in different intervals in the defined range of the data is different due to the effect of other variable, describe the sub-trends in piecewise manner (Check the 2021-6b example).

E.g.

a. “Based on the data for the 48-hour period, describe the effect of IL-19 on FXR1 gene expression”. (2020 practice2-2c)

  • IL-19 causes a small increase in expression of the FXR1 gene.

(relative amount of FXR1 protein at 0hr: 0.98 ± 0.070; relative amount of FXR1 protein at 48hr: 1.60 ± 0.520. Significant difference, with no significant difference in the neighboring data points in between that seemingly have a fluctuation in the sample means.)

b. “Describe the effect of amanitin on the maximum elongation rate for the wild-type and modified RNA polymerases.” (2020 practice 1-1c)

  • Amanitin decreases the maximum elongation rate for the wild strain and does not affect the rate in the experimental strain.

(Bar graphs with error bars provided; Two data points with error bars for comparison)

c. “Based on the data shown in Figure 2, describe the relationship between the concentration of ouabain and the Na+/K+ ATPase activity both in human kidney cells AND in PKD cells.” (2021-1c)

  • There is an inverse relationship/negative correlation between the concentration of ouabain and the ATPase activity (in both types of cells).
  • Increasing concentrations of ouabain result in decreasing ATPase activity in both cases.

(Line graph; > 3 data points)

d. “Based on the data in Table 1, describe the relationship between the level of CO2 and the average number of mitochondria per unit area of a cell.” (2023-2c)

  • The number of mitochondria is greater under conditions of elevated CO2 (than under normal CO2).
  • There is a positive correlation/relationship between the level of CO2 and the average number of mitochondria per unit area of a cell.

(Line graph; > 3 data points)

e. “Describe the trend in the average concentration of mRNA I throughout the experiment.” (2021-6b)

  • There is no change in concentration from 1 to 3 hours, increased slightly in concentration between 3 and 6 hours (during the heat shock), increased concentration at a greater rate from 6 to 10 hours, and decreased concentration after hour 10.

(Description for different intervals in a range of independent variables)
