# Unit 1 Chemistry of Life

## 1. Water

-	Polar covalent bond & hydrogen bond:
-	Electronegativity difference between H and O atoms (but not large enough to cause e- transfer)
-	Unequal electron sharing between H and O  forms polar covalent bond in a molecule
-	H is partially positive and O is partially negative
-	H in one water molecule attracts O in another  hydrogen bond (strongest intermolecular interaction)
-	Polar molecules attracts water  hydrophilic

	Charged: resulted from electron transfer. Attracts water  hydrophilic
	Nonpolar: resulted from equal electron sharing or bond polarities canceling out. Repels water  hydrophobic

	Properties of water
	Thermal properties
	High specific heat: no change in state, change in temperature; aquatic systems
	High latent heat of vaporization: change in state, no change in temperature; evaporative cooling of terrestrial organisms.
	Solvent properties
	Hydrophilic (polar / charged)
	Hydrophobic (nonpolar)
	Cohesion & adhesion
	Cohesion: H bonds between water  surface tension
	Adhesion: H bonds between water & hydrophilic surface
	Transpiration – cohesion – adhesion – tension model (TCAT)
	Transpiration – water diffuses out of leaf through stomata due to water potential difference; forming tension in the xylem
	Cohesion – cohesion among water molecules in the xylem transmits the tension from leaf to root (lower water potential in leaf, higher in roots and soil)
	Adhesion – adhesion resists gravity, preventing water molecules from falling down
	Ice floating on water
	Stable hydrogen bond in ice space out water molecules, reducing density
	Water molecules moving around in liquid water create and break transient hydrogen bonds  smaller average distance between water molecules than in ice
	Insulate heat in winter, protecting aquatic organisms from freezing
	Nutrient turnover when ice thaws in spring

AP答题套路：How does mutation lead to loss of function in a protein?
Change DNA sequence  change amino acid sequence  affect interactions between amino acids  affect secondary and tertiary structure  change interactions between the functional domain (e.g. active site in enzymes / ligand-binding domain in receptors) and its target  disrupt functions
AP答题套路：How does change in pH / raise in temperature lead to loss of function in an enzyme?
Changes in pH / increase in temperature  affect interactions between amino acid R-groups, affecting particularly the hydrophobic interactions & hydrogen bonds  change folding of the enzyme  change conformation  change in the shape & charge of the active site  affect function
