Chapter 9 Brain States

  • Measure brain’s electrical activity → electroencephalography (EEG)脑电图
    • Records net electrical activity of thousands of neurons firing放电 simultaneously
    • “Brain waves脑波” → cyclic rising and falling electrical potentials
  • 2 main sleep states: slow wave sleep (SWS)慢波睡眠 and rapid eye movement sleep (REM)快速眼动睡眠
  • SWS → high amplitude波幅 and low frequency频率 brain waves
    • Slow waves become less frequent the longer one is asleep
  • Dreaming occurs during REM sleep
  • During REM sleep → much lower amplitudes and neuron activity is less synchronized同步的
  • Atonia张力缺失/弛缓 = loss of muscle tone, body becomes paralyzed
    • Except for muscles that regulate breathing and eye movements
  • Periods of SWS and REM sleep alternate in 90-minute cycles with 75–80 minutes of SWS

followed by 10–15 minutes of REM sleep

  • Arousal systems → in upper brainstem
    • Use neurotransmitters acetylcholine乙酰胆碱, norepinephrine去甲肾上腺素, serotonin血清素, and glutamate谷氨酸 → maintain wakefulness
  • Orexin-producing neurons → in hypothalamus
    • Orexin食欲素 → produce excitatory effects on arousal systems
    • Orexin-producing neurons → prevent sudden transitions to sleep
    • Orexin neurons activate hypothalamic neurons containing histamine组胺 → helps with staying awake
  • When awake → high acetylcholine and norepinephrine
  • During REM → norepinephrine remains low and acetylcholine is high
  • In SWS → low acetylcholine and norepinephrine
  • Active suppression of arousal systems → by ventrolateral preoptic (VLPO) nucleus腹外侧视前核 in hypothalamus
    • Cells in VLPO release inhibitory neurotransmitters galanin甘丙肽 and gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA)
    • Suppress arousal systems
    • Damage to VLPO nucleus → irreversible insomnia失眠
  • Circadian timing system → regulated by suprachiasmatic nucleus视交叉上核 in hypothalamus下丘脑
    • Functions as master clock
    • Cells express “clock proteins” called per and tim
  • Sleep factor → adenosine腺苷
    • When one stays awake → adenosine levels increase
    • Adenosine reduces arousal
    • Caffeine咖啡因 acts as stimulant → bind to adenosine receptors and prevent their interaction with adenosine
  • Most common sleep disorder → insomnia
  • Daytime sleepiness → excessive feelings of tiredness during the day
  • Sleep apnea睡眠呼吸暂停 → muscles of respiratory tract relax during sleep, closing airways
    • Breathing machine to keep airway open → continuous positive airway pressure持续气道正压通气 or “CPAP” machine
  • REM sleep behavior disorder → nerve pathways in brain that prevent muscle movement during REM sleep do not work
    • Patients act out their dreams
    • Common in people with degenerative diseases like Parkinson’s, stroke and other types of dementia
    • Symptoms treated drugs for Parkinson’s or with benzodiazepines苯二氮卓 like clonazepam氯硝西泮 (enhances effects of GABA)
  • Narcolepsy发作性睡病 is relatively uncommon sleep disorder
    • Loss of orexin neurons in lateral hypothalamus → help control transitions into sleep
    • Lead to sleep attacks during the day
    • Enter REM sleep very quickly
    • Patients can enter a dreaming state while still awake → hypnagogic hallucinations入睡前幻觉
    • Muscle tone loss while awake → cataplexy猝倒
  • Melanin-concentrating hormone黑色素聚集激素 induces sleep
    • Released from hypothalamus
  • Neurotransmitters: dopamine (for movement), norepinephrine (for alertness), serotonin (for emotion), acetylcholine, histamine (increase arousal)
  • Thalamus → regulates arousal, relay sensory inputs to cerebral cortex
  • Reticular activating system网状激活系统 in brainstem:
    • Coordinates signals coming from sensory inputs
    • Controls autonomic nervous system自主神经系统 → influences heart rate, blood flow, and breathing
  • Disorders that affect ability to pay attention: attention deficit hyperactivity disorder注意力缺失过动症 (ADHD), schizophrenia精神分裂, prosopagnosia人面失认症, and hemineglect syndrome半侧忽略综合症
  • Prosopagnosia = face blindness → people cannot recognize faces
  • Hemineglect syndrome → caused by damage to right parietal cortex which is important in involuntary attention
    • Patients with neglect ignore left side of visual field视野
