Chapter 16 Kinds of Research

  • Histology组织学 → study of how cells form tissues
  • Anatomy解剖学 → study of structure
  • Magnetic resonance imaging磁共振成像 → MRI
    • Gray matter → cell bodies of neurons + dendrites and synapses
    • White matter → axons wrapped in fatty myelin sheath
    • Based on distribution of water in tissues
  • To study neuronal activity → electrophysiology电生理学
  • Synaptic plasticity突触可塑性 → capacity of synapses to become stronger or weaker in response to sensory inputs or experiences
  • Electroencephalography脑电图 or EEG → record brain activity without invasive or harmful procedures
    • About 20 thin metal discs are placed on scalp
    • Records activity of neurons near brain surface
    • Help diagnose epilepsy癫痫 and identify different stages of sleep
  • Two-photon microscopy双光子显微技术 → help study individual living neurons
    • Animals are genetically modified to produce proteins that glows when laser beams shine on them
  • Adenine腺嘌呤 (A), cytosine胞嘧啶 (C), guanine鸟嘌呤 (G), and thymine胸腺嘧啶 (T) bases comprise 20,000 genes
  • DNA chips or microarrays微阵列 → identify variations in copy number
  • CRISPR (Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats) → by tethering DNA-cutting enzymes to targeting system can help create mutations found in patients with neurological disorders
  • Optogenetics光遗传学 → genetically modify a lab animal so that its neurons produce a light-responsive protein
    • Optical fibers光纤 are inserted into brain to allow light to shine on those neurons, either activating or silencing them
  • Lissencephaly无脑回畸形/平脑症 → brain malformation in which surface of brain becomes smooth without normal ridges and grooves
  • Kabuki syndrome歌舞伎综合症 → marked by intellectual disabilities, distinctive facial appearance, slow growth in infancy
    • Have mutations in KMT2D gene
  • Fragile X syndrome脆性X综合症 → most common form of congenital先天的 intellectual disability in males
    • Caused by excessive number of CGG repeats within FMR1 gene
      • FMR1→ important for synapse function
    • Sea slug Aplysia海兔 → used in studies of learning and memory
    • Fruit fly Drosophila → used to study behavior and how different genes mediate behavior
    • Microdialysis微透析 → help monitor neurotransmitters in action
      • Help collect small volumes of liquid from just outside neurons
      • Can also be used to deliver substances to brain
    • Mass spectrometry质谱法 → compounds are ionized (given an electric charge) and then sent through electric or magnetic field
      • Behavior of each molecule in that field indicates its mass → help identify molecules
    • Functional MRI (fMRI) 功能磁共振成像 → follow changes in brain activity and function
      • Detects blood flow and differences in oxygen-rich and oxygen-poor blood
        • More active regions → need more oxygen and nutrients and more blood
      • Magnetoencephalography (MEG)脑磁图 → detects actual electrical currents coursing through groups of neurons
        • When neuron activities are synchronized同步化 → combined electrical currents produce weak magnetic fields
        • Help detect rapid changes in brain activity (temporal resolution时间分辨率) but it does not have good spatial resolution
          • Can combine MEG data with fMRI data for anatomical detail
        • Near-infrared spectroscopy (NIRS) 近红外光谱学 → monitors flow of oxygenated blood
          • Help estimate neuron activity
          • Only useful for measuring activity near surface of brain and does not provide as much anatomical detail
          • Far less expensive and cumbersome than fMRI
          • Portable → NIRS useful for studying brain activity during various tasks
        • Positron emission tomography (PET) 正电子发射计算机断层 → detects short-lived radioactive compounds that have been injected into bloodstream
          • Radioactive compounds include labelled oxygen, glucose, and neurotransmitters
          • PET has good temporal resolution like MEG
            • But lacks detailed spatial resolution of MRI
          • Transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS)经颅磁刺激 → generates magnetic field which near patient’s head
            • TMS is used to treat psychiatric disorders, including
              • Anxiety焦虑症
              • Depression抑郁症
              • Post-traumatic stress disorder创伤后应激障碍
