Chapter 14 Injury and Illness

  • Brain Tumors
    • Tumors that originate in brain → primary brain tumors 原发性脑肿瘤
      • Most common type of primary brain tumor → meningioma 脑膜瘤
        • Meninges 脑膜 → thin protective layers of tissue that cover brain
      • Metastatic 远端转移 brain tumors → spread from tumors of lungs, breast, skin, colon, or kidney
      • Gliomas 神经胶质瘤 → tumors that develop from glial cells that insulate and protect neurons in brain
      • Most common form of brain cancer → glioblastoma 胶质母细胞瘤
      • Growing gliomas release glutamate谷氨酸 → neurotoxic at high concentrations and destroy nerve cells, may cause seizures
      • Craniotomy 开颅术 → skull is opened and tumor is removed
      • Stereotactic radiosurgery立体定向放疗 → high dose of radiation is aimed precisely at tumor
      • Steroid类固醇 medications → reduce swelling in brain
      • Immunotherapy 免疫治疗 → stimulate immune system to decrease tumor growth
      • Stem cells 干细胞 → undifferentiated cells that can differentiate分化 into any type of cells
    • Traumatic brain injuries (TBI) 创伤性脑损伤
      • Falls → leading cause of traumatic brain injury
      • Motor vehicle/traffic injury → leading cause of TBI-related death
    • People who sustain repeated concussions脑震荡 and other brain trauma → can develop progressive degenerative退行性 brain disease called chronic traumatic encephalopathy (CTE) 慢性创伤性脑病
    • Spinal cord injury (SCI) 脊髓损伤
      • Methylprednisolone甲泼尼龙 is steroid drug → only treatment for SCI
        • If administered within 8 hours of injury → effective in treating SCI
      • Neurological acquired immune deficiency syndrome 获得性免疫缺陷综合征
        • Human immunodeficiency virus 人类免疫缺陷性病毒 (HIV) → cause acquired immunodeficiency syndrome (AIDS) 艾滋病
        • More than half of people with HIV develop HIV-associated neurocognitive disorders (HAND) HIV 相
          关神经认知障碍
          • Cause difficulty with concentration, memory, coordination, decision-making → AIDS dementia 艾滋病痴呆综合征
        • Peripheral neuropathy周边神经病变 involves injury to nerves of limbs四肢
          • Cause discomfort ranging from tingling麻刺感 and burning to severe pain
        • Multiple Sclerosis多发性硬化
          • Inflammatory autoimmune disease 自体免疫性疾病 of central nervous system
          • Diagnosed in people between 20 and 40 years of age
          • Target → myelin sheath髓鞘, protective coat found around nerve fibers
          • 5 times more prevalent in temperate climates
          • Caucasians 高加索人 have higher risk
          • Symptoms → numbness, clumsiness, and blurred vision视力模糊, slurred speech口齿不清, weakness, pain, loss of coordination, uncontrollable tremors, loss of bladder control膀胱失控, memory loss, general fatigue疲劳
            • Relapsing-remitting MS 复发缓解型→ flare-ups 突然爆发 of new or worsening symptoms followed by complete or partial remission 缓解 of symptoms
            • Primary-progressive MS原发性进展型/初发恶化型 → progressive worsening of symptoms after disease onset
            • Secondary-progressive MS 后续恶化型 → relapsing-remitting disease has transitioned into a progressive form
          • Steroid drugs, like glucocorticoids 糖皮质激素→ reduce inflammation and help shorten and reduce acute 急性 attacks
        • Chronic Pain慢性疼痛
          • Anesthesia 麻醉 → used to prevent pain during medical procedures
            • Commonly used local anesthetics 局部麻醉剂 include procaine 普鲁卡因(Novocain诺瓦卡因) and lidocaine利多卡因
          • 4 types of painkillers:
            • Aspirin 阿司匹林and nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs 非类固醇消炎药 (NSAIDs) such as ibuprofen 布洛芬 and naproxen萘普生
          • Opioids 阿片类药物 such as morphine 吗啡 and codeine可待因
          • Antiepileptic抗癫痫 agents such as gabapentin加巴喷丁
          • Antidepressants 抗抑郁药 such as amitriptyline阿米替林
        • NSAIDs → inhibit pain-producing chemicals (including prostaglandins前列腺素)
        • Antiepileptic and antidepressant drugs → used to treat nerve pain that results from injury to nervous system → neuropathy神经病变
        • Naturally occurring opioids → endorphins内啡肽
        • Opioid receptors → concentrated in spinal cord脊髓
        • Stroke中风
        • Stroke → interruption in blood flow to brain
          • Hemorrhagic 大出血→ ruptured 破裂 blood vessel
          • Ischaemic 局部缺血→ blood clots clog up blood vessels
        • Leading cause of long-term disability
        • Tissue plasminogen activator 组织凝血酶原激活剂 (tPA) → clot-dissolving 溶栓 drug approved by FDA
          • Helps to break down and dissolve blood clots
