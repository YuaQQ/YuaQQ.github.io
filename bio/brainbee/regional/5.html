Chapter 5 Thinking, Planning and Language

  • Concept cells概念细胞 reside in temporal lobe → specializes in object recognition物体识别
  • Problems with recognizing and identifying visual stimuli → agnosia失认
  • Fusiform face area (FFA)梭状回面孔区
    • Located on underside of temporal lobe
    • Critical for recognizing faces
    • Bilateral damage to this area → prosopagnosia人面失认症 or “face blindness”脸盲症
  • Written language → visual word form area (VWFA)视觉词形成区 in fusiform gyrus梭状回 recognizes written letters and words
  • Regions that encode words include:
    • Posterior parietal cortex后顶叶皮层
    • Temporal lobe
    • Prefrontal cortex (PFC)
  • Together form semantic system语义系统 → responds more strongly to words than to other sounds
  • Accounts for humans’ unique ability to use language
  • Occipitotemporal枕颞 cortex → participates in visual recognition and reading
    • Help identify shape of written numbers or symbols and connect them to their meaning
  • Language disorders → aphasias失语症
  • Broca’s area布洛卡区 → speech production
    • Broca’s aphasia, also called “non-fluent” aphasia非流利型失语 → speech production is impaired but comprehension is intact
    • Speech is slow and halting, requires great effort, lacks complexity
  • Wernicke’s area → responsible for understanding speech
    • Wernicke’s aphasia, or “fluent aphasia”流利型失语
      • Speech is riddled with errors in sound and word selection, may be unintelligible
    • Pure word deafness → caused by damage to superior temporal lobes in both hemispheres
      • Comprehension of heard speech on any level is affected
      • Can hear speech, music, and other sounds, and can detect tones and emotions
      • Cannot link sounds of words to their meaning
    • Language-associated gene → FOXP2
      • Mutations in FOXP2 lead to impairments of mouth and jaw movements
        • Difficulty with spoken and written language
      • FOXP2 affects signaling in dorsal striatum背侧纹状体
        • Neurons in dorsal striatum express high levels of product of FOXP2
          • Regulate signaling between motor and speech regions of brain
        • Prefrontal cortex (PFC) → last region of brain to develop
        • Executive functions = a blend of 3 core skills: inhibition, working memory and shifting
        • Inhibition → ability to suppress behaviors or actions when they are inappropriate
        • Working memory depends on both PFC and parietal lobe
          • Transient form of declarative or conscious memory
        • Shifting, or mental flexibility, allows to adjust ongoing behaviors when conditions require it
        • Brain regions related to motivation and emotion → amygdala and nucleus accumbens
        • Orbitofrontal cortex眶额叶皮层 → region of PFC located just behind eyes → important for affective decision-making in situations involving reward and punishment
          • Implicated in addiction and social behavior
        • Social neuroscience → study of neural functions that underlie interpersonal behavior: reading and understanding social cues and norms, choosing socially-appropriate responses, and understanding oneself and others
          • Medial PFC → involved in these skills
        • Temporoparietal junction (TPJ) 颞顶联合区 → focus on others and not on the self
        • “Mirror neurons” 镜像神经元 → fired when monkeys watch another person or monkey perform the same action
