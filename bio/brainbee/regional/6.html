Chapter 6 The Developing Brain

  • Induction → Neurogenesis (cell division) → Migration（神经元）迁移
    • Cell division = proliferation
  • Migration → newly formed neurons travel to their final destinations
  • 3 layers emerge → ectoderm外胚层 (outermost layer), mesoderm中胚层 (middle layer), and endoderm内胚层 (inner-most layer)
  • Signals from mesoderm trigger ectoderm cells to become nerve tissue → neural induction神经诱导
  • Sonic hedgehog音猬因子 → secreted from mesodermal tissue (notochord and floor plate) lying beneath spinal cord
    • Adjacent nerve cells → glia
    • Cells that are farther away → motor neurons
    • Even lower concentration → interneurons中间神经元
  • Excessive proliferation of brain cells → megalencephaly巨脑症 — brain is abnormally large and heavy
  • Migration begins 3 to 4 weeks after conception
  • Head end of neural tube forms 3 bulges → hindbrain后脑 (rhombencephalon菱脑), midbrain (mesencephalon中脑), and forebrain (prosencephalon前脑)
  • At week 7 → signs of eyes and hemispheres appear
  • Most common guidance mechanism (about 90% of migration) → radial glia放射状胶质细胞
    • Primary neural stem cells (NSCs)
    • Occurs in “inside-out” manner:
      • Cells that arrive earliest (oldest ones) form deepest layers of cerebral cortex
      • Late-arriving (youngest) neurons form outermost layers
    • Sideways, or tangential, migration切向迁移 → cells move parallel to brain’s surface
    • Exposure to alcohol, cocaine可卡因, or radiation → affect proper migration
    • Growth cones生长锥 → enlargements at tips of axons
      • Actively explore environment
      • Guided to that final destination by molecular cues
        • Such as netrin轴突生长诱向因子, semaphorin臂板蛋白, and ephrin肝配蛋白
          • Netrin was first discovered in a worm
        • Astrocytes exert their influence on synaptic development and function
        • Myelination髓鞘形成 → fatty wrapping of axons
          • Increases — by as much as 100 times — speed of action potentials
        • Nodes of Ranvier郎飞氏结/兰氏结 → interrupt myelin sheath
        • Saltatory conduction跳跃传导 (“leaping”) → responsible for more rapid transmission of electrical potentials
        • Formation of myelin occurs throughout lifespan
        • Only about half of neurons generated during development survive
        • Apoptosis细胞凋亡 → programmed cell death
        • Nerve growth factor神经生长因子 → important for survival of sensory neurons
        • Pruning修剪 of excess connections → dependent on relative activity of each connection
          • Active connections → survive
          • Connections with little activity → lost
        • Astrocytes play a role in pruning
          • Astrocytes aid formation of eye-specific connections by engulfing and removing excessive synapses
