Chapter 8 Adult and Aging Brain

  • Between childhood and adulthood → brain loses gray matter because excess cells and synapses are pruned away
  • Around age 40, white matter reaches its peak volume
  • Most important brain area to become fully “wired up” in adulthood → prefrontal cortex前额叶皮质
  • Intelligence peaks during early to middle adulthood (ages 25 to 60)
    • Fluid intelligence流体智力 → abilities like problem solving and identifying patterns
      • Peaks around age 30
    • Crystallized intelligence晶体智力 → deals with vocabulary and basic knowledge of facts and figures
      • Increases until age 50
    • Dementia痴呆 → decline in cognitive ability which affects person’s day-to-day functioning
    • Pronounced decline in memory and cognitive ability → disease processes
      • Widespread and profound loss of neurons is not part of normal aging
    • Cognition认知 starts declining as early as 20s
    • Declarative memory陈述性记忆 → declines with age
    • Nondeclarative memory非陈述性记忆 → remains almost intact
    • Working memory工作记忆 = ability to hold a piece of information in mind for a short period of time; also declines with age
    • Prefrontal cortex, cerebellum, and hippocampus → biggest losses in advanced age
    • Cortical thinning皮质变薄 → pronounced in frontal lobes and parts of temporal lobes (hippocampus)
    • “Last in, first out后进先出” theory of brain aging → last parts of brain to develop are first to atrophy
    • Olfactory bulbs嗅球 & dentate gyrus齿状回 of hippocampus → adult neurogenesis
    • Free radicals自由基 = reactive molecules which destroy fats, proteins and DNA
    • Brain → one of most metabolically active organs
      • Needs around 20% of body’s fuel
    • Microglia小神经胶质细胞 = brain’s immune cells → defend against pathogens, clean up cellular debris, and help form synapses
