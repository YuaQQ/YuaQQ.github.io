Chapter 13 Addiction

  • Addiction 成瘾→ people cannot control use of drugs despite negative consequences
  • Tolerance 耐药性→ body gets used to drugs and requires more of them to achieve same effect
  • Withdrawal 戒断反应→ lack of drug causes body to react with unpleasant or life-threatening physical symptoms
    • Include headaches, muscle ache, vomiting, severe tremors震颤, seizures癫痫发作
  • Abused drugs induce pleasure by activating reward system
    • Controlled by dopamine多巴胺
    • Brain region involved → limbic system边缘系统
    • Reward system → generates habits and learned behaviors
  • About 50% of addiction can be traced to genetic factors
  • Opioids阿片类药物（类鸦片药物）
    • Opium 鸦片 (also known as morphine吗啡) obtained from juice of poppy flowers罂粟花
    • Heroin 海洛因→ opioid made by drying morphine, adding various chemicals, and then heating it to produce powder
    • Pituitary gland 脑垂体 secretes natural opioids → endorphins内啡肽
      • Help control motivation, emotion, alleviate pain and induce euphoria亢奋
    • Synthetic opioids → heroin and pain medicines codeine可待因, oxycodone 羟考酮 (oxycontin), and fentanyl芬太尼
      • Fentanyl used to treat severe pain
    • Medically important uses: suppress cough, inhibit diarrhea腹泻, relieve pain
    • In excess → cause breathing problems which is cause of death in overdose过量
    • “Antidote” → naloxone 纳洛酮 which interacts with opioid receptors without inducing biological effects
    • Methadone 美沙酮 and buprenorphine 丁丙诺啡 → stimulate opioid receptors
      • Reduce withdrawal symptoms
      • Buprenorphine is safer because it is weaker than methadone
    • Nicotine尼古丁
      • Nicotine → addictive substance in tobacco
      • Binds to nicotinic acetylcholine 烟碱型乙酰胆碱受体 receptors
      • Triggers release of adrenaline 肾上腺素→ causes ‘kick’ which leads to rapid heartbeat and raised blood pressure
      • Creates buzz of pleasure and energy → due to dopamine多巴胺
      • Useful in treatments for cognitive disorders such as schizophrenia 精神分裂症, ADHD注意力缺失过动症, and Alzheimer’s disease阿尔兹海默症
      • Tobacco → leading cause of preventable deaths in US
      • Nicotine → packaged into gum, skin patches, lozenges润喉含片, nasal sprays or inhalers
      • Buprenorphine 丁丙诺啡→ can help treat opioid addiction by simulating nicotine’s effects on dopamine
      • One of newest treatment → varenicline畅沛（酒石酸伐尼克兰片）
        • Nicotine mimic → attaches to nicotinic acetylcholine receptors
      • Alcohol
        • Cirrhosis 肝硬化→ scarring of liver
        • Ethanol 乙醇→ addictive substance of alcoholic beverages
        • At low doses → acts as stimulant
        • At higher blood levels → acts as depressant
          • Induce sleepiness and short-term memory loss
            • Targets GABA γ－ 氨基丁酸 receptors → activate brain’s inhibitory system
              • Calms anxiety, reduces reaction time
            • Blocks NMDA N-甲基-D-天冬氨酸受体 glutamate receptors → alter mood and impair memory
          • Stimulates pain-relief circuits → act on natural opioid molecules
          • Is diuretic 利尿剂 = chemical that pulls water from body tissues
          • People with alcohol use disorder → alcoholism酗酒
            • Have unsteady gait步态不稳, tremors震颤, and slurred speech口齿不清
              • Results from damage to cerebellum小脑
            • Disulfiram双硫仑, naltrexone纳曲酮, and acamprosate 阿坎酸→ used to treat alcohol addiction
          • Marijuana大麻
            • Also known as weed or pot
            • Comes from dried Cannabis 大麻 plant
            • Contains active chemical tetrahydrocannabinol四氢大麻酚, or THC → distorts perception and alters sense of time, space, and self
            • Natural form of THC → anandamide花生四烯乙醇胺
              • Works through cannabinoid 大麻素 receptors → normally help coordinate movement
            • Hippocampus海马体, involved in memory and learning → contains many THC receptors
            • Marijuana affects short-term memory短期记忆
            • Another active compound in marijuana → cannabidiol大麻二酚, or CBD
              • Does not produce highs
              • Can help control epileptic seizures癫痫发作, relieve pain and inflammation炎症, and can be used to treat mental illnesses and addictions
            • Psychostimulants精神兴奋剂
              • Boost physical and mental functions → nickname “speed”
              • Common psychostimulants → caffeine 咖啡因 and nicotine尼古丁
              • Doctors prescribe amphetamine 安非他命 (Adderall) and methylphenidate 哌甲酯 (Ritalin利他林 or Concerta专注达) to treat ADHD and sleeping disorder narcolepsy发作性睡病
              • Illegal psychostimulants → cocaine 可卡因 and methamphetamine 甲基苯丙胺, or “meth”
              • Smokable drugs → cocaine (crack霹雳可卡因，可卡因的游离碱形式) and crystal methamphetamine (crystal meth)
                • Produce rush of euphoria and feelings of power and self-confidence
              • Psychostimulants overwhelm reward system with dopamine
              • Treatments include immune cells (microglia小胶质细胞) and oxytocin催产素 — the “love” or “happiness” hormone
            • Club Drugs
              • Include 3,4-methylenedioxy-methamphetamine 3,4-亚甲基二氧基甲基苯丙胺(also known as MDMA, Ecstasy, or Molly), rohypnol 氟硝西泮 (“roofies”), GHB y-羟基丁酸(gamma hydroxybutyrate), and ketamine氯胺酮
            • Stimulants → ecstasy摇头丸
            • Depressants → rohypnol, GHB and ketamine
            • Ecstasy → boosts levels of serotonin血清素
            • Rohypnol and GHB are both depressants and mimic benzodiazepines like Valium
              • Known as “date-rape” drugs
            • Ketamine, called “Special K” → depressant that is legally used as anesthetic麻醉剂
              • Alters glutamate 谷氨酸signaling → non-traditional target for antidepressant medications
