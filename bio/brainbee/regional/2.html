Chapter 2 Senses & Perception

●        Visual system视觉系统 involves about 30% of cerebral cortex大脑皮层

●        Fruit flies (Drosophila) and mice → model animals used to study how light energy is converted into electrical signals

●        Iris虹膜 → regulates how much light enters into eyes by changing size of pupils瞳孔

●        Lens晶状体 → bends light so that it focuses on inner surface of eyeball

●        Visual input is mapped onto retina视网膜 as a 2D reversed image

●        Three-layered retina → 3 types of neurons

○     Photoreceptors光感受器, interneurons (bipolar cells双极细胞), and ganglion cells节细胞

●        Light must travel through ganglion cells and interneurons before it reaches photoreceptors

●        Axons of ganglion cells → form optic nerve视神经

●        There are 125 million photoreceptors in each human eye

●        Process of converting one form of energy into another → transduction

●        Rods视杆细胞 make up about 95% of photoreceptors

○       Extremely sensitive → enable to see in dim light

●        Cones视锥细胞 → visual acuity and colors

○       Eye contains 3 types of cones (red, green and blue)

●        Central part of retina → fovea中央凹

○       Cones are most densely packed

○       Only red and green cones

●        Area around fovea = macula黄斑 → important for reading and driving

●        Degeneration of photoreceptors in macula = macular degeneration黄斑变性 → leading cause of blindness in people older than 55

●        In macular region visual acuity is highest,

○       Each ganglion cell receives input from just 1 cone → resolve extremely fine details

●        Part of visual space providing input to single ganglion cell = receptive field感受野

●        Center-surround antagonism → maximizes perception of contrast

●        Exit point of optic nerve → forms blind spot盲点

●        Signals from both eyes converge at optic chiasm视交叉

●        Visual pathway:

○     Retina → Optic nerves → Optic chiasm → Optic tracts视束 → Lateral geniculate nucleus外侧膝状体 (thalamus) → Optic radiation视觉辐射 → Primary visual cortex初级视觉皮层

●        Seeing with two eyes = binocular vision双眼视觉 → allows to perceive depth

●        Information from left visual field end up on right side of brain and vice versa

●        Crossed eyes → condition called strabismus斜视

○     People miss out on depth perception

○     Research with cats and monkeys has helped find therapies for strabismus

■        In strabismus, beyond age of 8 blindness becomes irreversible

●        Left side of brain → understanding and producing speech

●        Damage to left auditory cortex (Wernicke’s area韦尼克区) → person cannot understand language

●        3 sound-amplifying bones in middle ear: malleus (hammer)锤骨, incus (anvil)砧骨, and stapes (stirrup)镫骨

●        Stapes acts like a tiny piston

●        Oval window椭圆窗 → separates air-filled middle ear from fluid-filled cochlea (inner ear)

○     Converts mechanical vibrations into pressure waves in fluid of cochlea

○     Then converted into electrical signals by receptor cells (hair cells毛细胞)

●        Basilar membrane基底膜 detects different frequencies (pitches)

○     Higher-pitched sounds → near oval window

○     Lower-pitched sounds → in the center and tip

●        Hair cells → excite auditory nerve听神经

○     Sends electrical signals to brainstem脑干

●        Then to thalamus → relay station for sensory information

●        Loss of hair cells → hearing loss

●        Sense of taste = gustation味觉 and of smell = olfaction嗅觉

●        Olfactory bulb嗅球 → in forebrain and is responsible for processing smell information

●        Olfactory neurons → are continually replaced throughout life

●        5,000 - 10,000 taste buds味蕾

○     Start to lose them around age 50

●        Each taste bud has 50 to 100 sensory cells

●        Can detect five basic taste qualities: sweet, sour, salty, bitter, and umami鲜味 (Japanese for “savory”)

○     Detected across the tongue and are not limited to specific regions

●        Gustatory signals sent through three cranial nerves脑神经/颅神经 — facial面神经, glossopharyngeal舌咽神经, and vagus nerves迷走神经

●        Impulses are routed through thalamus to gustatory cortex in frontal lobe and insula脑岛 where specific taste perceptions are analyzed

●        Smell → sent directly to cerebral cortex without first passing through thalamus

●        There are around 1,000 different types of olfactory cells嗅细胞 → can identify about 20 times as many smells

●        Primary olfactory cortex初级嗅觉皮层 located in temporal lobe

●        Olfactory bulbs in rodents and primates (including humans) → produce new neurons (neurogenesis神经发生) throughout life

●        Somatosensory system躯体感觉系统 → control touch sensations

○     Including light touch, pressure, vibration, temperature, texture, itch, and pain

●        Skin → main sense organ for touch

●        Touch information travels quickly along myelinated fibers有髓神经纤维 with thick axons (A-beta fibers) or more slowly along thin, unmyelinated axons无髓轴突 (C fibers)

●        Sensitivity of different body parts depends largely on number of receptors per unit area and distance between them

●        To measure sensitivity → use two-point discrimination

○     Minimum distance between two points on skin that a subject identifies as distinct stimuli

●        Pain detected by nociceptors伤害感受器

●        Sensitive to different stimuli:

○     Thermal (heat or cold)

○     Mechanical (wounds)

○     Chemical (toxins or venoms)

●        Capsaicin辣椒素 in hot peppers → produce burning pain

●        Histamine组胺 → cause itch and is involved in skin irritation, insect bites, and allergies过敏

●        Prostaglandins前列腺素 → enhance sensitivity of receptors to tissue damage

●        In allodynia触诱发痛 → non-noxious stimulus produce pain

●        Hypersensitivity → perceived pain is prolonged → called neuropathic pain神经病理性疼痛

○     Diabetic neuropathy糖尿病神经病变 → nerves damaged by prolonged exposure to blood sugars

■        Cause numbness, tingling or burning pain

●        A-delta fibers → evoke immediate and sharp pain

●        Unmyelinated C fibers → produce dull and diffuse pain

●        Pain information sent to a region of brainstem called periaqueductal gray matter导水管周围灰质

○     Activates descending pathways that modulate pain

○     Trigger release of endorphins内啡肽 → mimic analgesic morphine

●        Adrenaline肾上腺素 → release in stressful situations

○     Works as analgesic镇痛剂 → relieves pain without a loss of consciousness

●          Opioid drugs can be injected into spinal cord to alleviate pain
