Chapter 4 Learning, Memory and Emotions

  • Henry Molaison (patient H.M.) had severe seizures
    • Had his medial temporal lobes内侧颞叶 removed — including hippocampi海马
    • Seizures abated, but caused permanent amnesia失忆症
  • Hippocampus and parahippocampal海马旁 region → convert memories from short-term to long-term
  • Medial temporal lobe includes hippocampus and parahippocampal regions
    • Form, organize, consolidate, and retrieve memories
  • Emotional or behavioral memories → in amygdala杏仁核, striatum纹状体, and cerebellum小脑
  • Dentate gyrus齿状回 → part of hippocampus海马 responsible for memories of events
    • Neurogenesis神经发生 takes place throughout lifetime
    • In learning and memory
  • Declarative memory陈述性记忆 → memory for facts, data, and events
    • Can be semantic or episodic
      • Semantic memories语义记忆 → knowledge, ideas, and concepts
      • Episodic memories情景记忆 → personal experiences and autobiographical自传的 events
    • Emotional significance of memories → mediated by amygdala
      • Amygdala modulates “fight-or-flight” responses战逃反应
    • Working memory工作记忆 → temporary type of declarative memory
      • Form of short-term memory
      • Coordinated by prefrontal cortex (PFC)前额叶
        • Also controls attention, decision-making, and long-term planning
      • Spatial memory空间记忆 → “place cells”位置细胞 in hippocampus
        • Create mental maps
      • “Grid cells”网格细胞
        • Located in entorhinal cortex内嗅皮质 close to hippocampus
        • Represent coordinates that allow brain to track position in space when landmarks aren’t present
      • Nondeclarative memory非陈述性记忆 = implicit or procedural memory内隐记忆/程序性记忆 → help perform learned motor skills
      • Parts of brain implicated:
        • Basal ganglia → “habit center” of brain
        • Prefrontal cortex
        • Cerebellum → motor control and coordination
      • Ability of synapses to remodel themselves = synaptic plasticity突触可塑性
      • Encoding new long-term memory involves changes in:
        • number and shape of synapses
        • amount of neurotransmitter released
        • number of receptors on postsynaptic membrane
      • Sea slug, Aplysia californica → animal model for studying synaptic plasticity
      • Molecules important for long-term memories:
        • N-methyl-d-aspartate (NMDA)
        • cAMP-response element binding protein (CREB)
          • CREB acts in nucleus of neurons and switch on genes
        • Two processes of synaptic plasticity: long-term potentiation (LTP) 长时程增强 and long-term depression (LTD) 长时程抑制
          • LTP is long-lasting increase in synaptic strength → in hippocampus
          • LTD decreases synapse’s effectiveness
        • Glutamate is the most prevalent neurotransmitter
          • NMDA
            • Ion channel
            • Permit calcium to flow into cells
          • AMPA (α-amino-3- hydroxy-5-methyl-4-isoxazolepropionic acid)
            • Ion channel
            • Permit sodium ions to flow into cells
          • Calcium ions → second messengers
            • Activates kinase proteins in case of LTP or phosphatases in LTD
            • Neurotrophins神经营养因子 are produced → stimulate growth of synapses and structural elements
          • Declarative memories are encoded in hippocampus → transferred to frontal lobes额叶 for long-term storage and consolidation巩固
          • Oxytocin催产素 → brain chemical linked to love
          • 6 basic emotions: anger愤怒, fear恐惧, surprise惊喜, disgust厌恶, joy快乐, sadness悲伤
          • Brain structures most closely linked to emotions: amygdala, insula or insular cortex岛叶皮质, and periaqueductal gray (located in midbrain)
          • Amygdala → integrates emotions (usually negative), emotional behavior, and motivation
            • Classical conditioning经典条件反射, in which stimulus is associated with reward or punishment → depends on amygdala
          • Insula → experience disgust — negative reaction to unpleasant stimulus
            • Also implicated in feeling and anticipating pain
          • Periaqueductal gray → pain perception and stress responses including defensive and reproductive behaviors, maternal attachment, and anxiety
            • Receptors for pain-reducing drugs like morphine and oxycodone are clustered in periaqueductal gray
          • Dopaminergic neurons多巴胺能神经元: voluntary movement随意运动, mood, reward, addiction, and memory
          • Reward pathway奖赏回路 → hippocampus, amygdala, and prefrontal cortex
            • k.a. mesolimbic pathway中脑边缘通路 → major pathway for dopamine
            • Connect midbrain’s ventral tegmental area (VTA)腹侧被盖区 to nucleus accumbens伏隔核
            • Cognitive processing of rewards and motivation
          • Lateral habenula外侧缰核 → encode punishment by inhibiting dopamine release
            • Dysfunction of lateral habenula → inappropriate aggression
