Chapter 1 Brain Basics

  • Central nervous system → brain脑 and spinal cord脊髓
  • Peripheral nervous system → nerves神经 and ganglia神经节 (gray matter)
  • Largest part of human brain → cerebrum大脑
  • A bridge between cerebral hemispheres → corpus callosum胼胝体
  • Frontal lobe额叶 → coordinate voluntary body movements and speech production, memory and emotions, higher cognitive skills such as problem-solving and planning, different aspects of personality
  • Parietal lobes顶叶 → integrate somatosensory information from skin, process taste and some types of visual information
  • Occipital lobes枕叶 → process visual inputs, help recognize colors and shapes
  • Temporal lobes颞叶 → analyze auditory information
  • Hippocampus海马 → encodes and consolidates new memories
  • Amygdala杏仁核 → integrates emotional memories
    • Hippocampus and amygdala belong to limbic system边缘系统 → regulate emotion and motivation
  • Thalamus丘脑 → integrates sensory information, serves as relay station
  • Hypothalamus下丘脑 → sends hormonal signals to pituitary gland
  • Midbrain中脑 → coordinate eye reflexes such as blinking and focusing, control auditory reflexes
    • Can also inhibit unwanted body movements and exerts fine motor control
  • Basal ganglia基底神经节 → help regulate body movements
  • Hindbrain后脑 → regulates blood sugar levels, sleep (via reticular formation) and control body movements
    • Cerebellum小脑 → second-largest part of brain in volume, contain over half of brain’s neurons
      • Coordinates voluntary movements
      • Controls acquisition of new motor skills
      • Cerebellar damage → jerky and arrhythmic gait, uncoordinated and imprecise body movements
  • Pons脑桥 → controls breathing
  • Medulla oblongata延髓 → regulates basic functions like blood pressure, heart rate, and breathing as well as protective reflexes like swallowing, choking, coughing
  • Brainstem脑干 = midbrain, pons, and medulla
  • Anterior commissure前连合 → transmits information between left and right temporal lobes
  • Corpus callosum → thick bundle of axons linking left and right cerebral hemispheres
  • Group of nerve tracts that connect different regions in brain → neural network神经网络
  • Electroencephalograph (EEG)脑电图 → measure brain waves
    • 4 distinct types:
    • Awake brain → alpha and beta waves
      • Alpha waves
        • Arise in parietal and occipital lobes
        • Frequencies between 8 and 13 Hz
      • Beta waves
        • Frequencies between 14 and 30 Hz
        • In frontal and parietal regions
  • Theta and delta waves → sleep
    • Theta waves
    • 4 to 7 Hz
    • Delta waves
    • During deep sleep
    • Less than 3.5 Hz
  • Alpha and delta waves → higher amplitude (20–200 μV) than beta or theta waves (5-10 μV)
  • Spinal tracts脊髓束 → chains of neurons that transmit electrical signals between brainstem and spinal cord
  • 80% of neurons → excitatory
    • Most common excitatory neurons → pyramidal cells锥体细胞
  • 20% of brain’s neurons are inhibitory → suppress activity of neurons
  • Seizure disorders癫痫 like epilepsy → imbalances between excitatory and inhibitory neurons
  • Dendrites树突 → receive signals from other neurons
  • Junctions between neurons = synapses突触
  • Axons轴突 → send signals to other cells
  • Axon terminals release chemical messengers = neurotransmitters神经递质
  • Functional unit of neural circuits and networks → neuron神经元
  • Cell body = soma胞体
  • Glia to neuron ratio is 1:1
  • CNS contains 4 types of glial cells: astrocytes星形胶质细胞, microglia小神经胶质细胞, ependymal cells室管膜细胞, and oligodendrocytes少突胶质细胞
    • Astrocytes → regulate ion and neurotransmitter concentrations, provide nutrients and oxygen, form blood brain barrier, help form new connections between neurons
    • Microglia → immune cells that phagocytose, help form new neuronal connections
    • Ependymal cells → produce cerebrospinal fluid which cushions brain
    • Oligodendrocytes → produce a fatty sheath around axons called myelin
  • Ions离子 → electrically charged atoms that enter cells through ion channels离子通道
  • Resting neuron → -70 mV, more negative inside the cell
  • Membrane potential less negative = depolarized去极化
  • Membrane potential more negative = hyperpolarized超极化
  • Electrical impulse which arises in axon = action potential动作电位
  • Signals are passed from one neuron to next at synapses
  • Calcium ions → exocytosis胞吐 of synaptic vesicles
  • Axon terminal doesn’t contain ribosomes to build proteins
  • Peptide-based neurotransmitters → built in ribosome-rich cell body
  • Golgi apparatus高尔基体 → protein-packaging organelle
  • Kinesins驱动蛋白 move along microtubules and carry synaptic vesicles for exocytosis
  • Postsynaptic density has a high concentration of neurotransmitter receptors
  • Ionotropic receptors离子通道型受体 → linked to ion channels
  • Metabotropic receptors代谢型受体 → receptor and ion channel are different proteins, but they are connected by a second messenger cascade
  • Neurotransmitters → either broken down or reabsorbed by axon terminals in process called reuptake再摄取
  • Excitatory neurons make neurotransmitters that open ion channels that depolarize dendrite’s membrane
  • Inhibitory neurons make neurotransmitters that hyperpolarize membrane
  • Brain’s most common excitatory neurotransmitter → glutamate谷氨酸
    • Binds to AMPA or NMDA receptors
      • AMPA receptors → fast and brief response
      • NMDA receptors activate more slowly
  • Important for learning and memory
  • Brain’s most common inhibitory neurotransmitter → gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA)γ-氨基丁酸
    • Ionotropic GABA receptors permeate negatively charged chloride ions
    • Metabotropic GABA receptors open ion channels that release potassium ions
  • Neuromodulators神经调质 → endocannabinoids内源性大麻素, cannabis-like chemicals that suppress neurotransmitter release
  • Hormones → affect distant tissues
    • Steroid hormones → estradiol雌二醇 or cortisol皮质醇
    • Form transcription factors that enter cell nucleus
  • Prostaglandins前列腺素 → small lipids that increase pain sensitivity and inflammation
  • Chromatin → complex of proteins and DNA
  • Tay-Sachs disease → fatal degenerative neurological condition
    • Caused by mutations in a gene that codes for beta-hexosaminidase A β-己糖胺酶 A
